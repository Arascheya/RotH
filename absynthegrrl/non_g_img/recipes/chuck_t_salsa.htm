Ingredients:

Chile Paste

6 (or so) dried Ancho chiles (one small package)

2 dried Guajillo chiles

4 dried Cascabel chiles

Fresh Ingredients

1 lb (about 10 medium) tomatillos

2 Roma tomatoes

1 Fresh Pobalno chile ("Pasilla" in market)

3 Fresh Serrano chiles - this is where the real heat comes from so adjust accordingly. 3 is medium heat, 6 es muy macho!

1 Medium white onion

1 head of garlic

2 tbsp salt (prox.)

Lime juice (optional)

Directions:

Tomatillo Salsa (Chile)

Make chile paste

Cut open dried chiles (Ancho, Guajillo & Cascabel) and remove stems, seeds and veins. Open them up flat on a hot, dry skillet over medium heat. Roast on skillet for 15 seconds or so while pressing them flat with a spatula. The dry flesh will blister and become aromatic, stop before they burn or turn black. Immediately drop the roasted chiles in a bowl of hot (not boiling) water and keep submerged for ½ hour. Blend the re-hydrated chiles in a blender with enough water to make a thick paste. Press the paste through a strainer with a rubber spatula, add water as needed to get the paste through. Skin will be left behind.

This makes more than enough chile paste for the salsa.

Make the Salsa

Peel and wash Tomatillos. Peel all the cloves of garlic. Peel and slice the onion into ½ inch slices

Dry roast Tomatillos, tomatos, onion, garlic and serranos on a skillet over medium heat until they are partially cooked and have block spots on them from the skillet.

Tomatillos and tomatoes take the longest. Peel the tomatos after they are roasted

Roast the onion slices on both sides till carmelized

Turn the garlic frequently till evenly roasted and spotted

Roast serranos till skin is dark and blistered, turning frequently. Peel them if you can then cut off the stem

BBQ or cook Pobalno (Pasilla) chile over an open flame till skin is completely blistered and black in spots. Remove skin and open the chile to remove the stem and seeds.

Place all roasted ingredients in a blender and bring to a pulpy consistency.

Add enough chile paste to flavor the salsa and to give it a brick-brown color.

Add salt in big pinches until the salsa looses the "vegetably" taste.

Add lime juice if you want a citrus twist, the tomatillos are already very tart.

Add water if it is too thick.

Pour into canning jars, close lids finger-tight, and cook fully submerged in boiling water for 10 minutes.
