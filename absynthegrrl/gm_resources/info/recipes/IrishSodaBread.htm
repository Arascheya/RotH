Irish Soda Bread

Ingredients:

1 cup all-purpose flour

3 cups coarse whole wheat flour, such as Odlum'd or Howard's

1 tablespoon wheat germ

1 tablespoon oat bran

1 tablespoon brown sugar

2 teaspoons baking soda

large egg

4 tablspoons unsalted butter, melted

1 1/2 to 1 3/4 cups buttermilk

2 tablespoons sesame seeds

Directions:

Preheat oven to 375. Butter and flour 9x5x3 loaf pan.

In a large bow, stir together dry ingrediants. Make a well in the center. With a wooden spoon, stir in the egg, melted butter, and 3/4 cup buttermilk. Mix well and gradually add enough remaining buttermilk to form a soft dough.

Sppon the dough into the prepared pan, smooth the top with a spatula that has been dipped in water or buttermilk, and sprinkle with sesame seeds. Bake 35 to 40 minutes, or until a skewer inserted in the center comes out clean and the bread sounds hollow when tapped on the bottom. Remove from the oven and let cool in the pan on a wire rack for 10 minutes. Turn out onto the rack and let it cool, right side up, for about an hour to make slicing easier.
