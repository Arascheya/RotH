BIGOS - The Polish Palate - try yourself

Bigos is one of the most representative dishes of Polish cuisine. It is made of sweet cabbage and sauerkraut (after a controlled fermentation process), several kinds of smoked and raw meats, and specially selected ingredients-dried plums, wild mushrooms, juniper berries, peppercorns, onion and sour apples. This concoction is then seasoned with honey and red wine. Fast food fans beware-bigos needs no fewer than three days to reach full flavor.

Ingredients: 1 kg sweet (white) cabbage, 1 kg sauerkraut, 800 ml water, 500 g roast pork, 500 g roast beef, 600 g sausage (variety), 400 g boiled ham, 2 tablespoon lard, 10 dried boletus mushroom, 10 stoned prunes, 10 crushed juniper berries, 10 peppercorns, 1 bay leaf, 2 onions, 2 cooking apples, 150 ml dry red wine, 1 teaspoon honey, salt, pepper.

1. Drain the excess juices from the sauerkraut and chop it finally. Chop the cabbage, pour boiling water over it and drain. Rinse the mushrooms; cover with a little warm water and leave to soak for 15 minutes. Cook the mushrooms in their soaking liquid for 30 minutes, drain and cut into strips. Keep the mushroom stock.

2. Peel the apples and cut into cubes. Peel and wash the onions then lightly fry them in 1 tablespoon of lard.

3. Put the cabbage and sauerkraut into a large saucepan. Add onions, mushrooms stock, apples, prunes, juniper berries, bay leaf and pepper. Add 800 ml of boiling water, cover and cook over a low heat for 1 and 1 hour. Cut the meats into cubes. Slice the sausage and fry it lightly in lard. Add all to the pan. Pour the wine in, and honey and cook for a further 40 minutes. Stir frequently taking care not to burn it (roast duck or venison and roasting gravies can be added to bigos).

Serve hot with wholemeal bread (bigos can be reheated for several days. Its flavour improves as it matures tastes best on the third day).
