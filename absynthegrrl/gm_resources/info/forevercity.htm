Forever City

Forever City, one of the episodes of the Shadow Journey games, came with a world editor. Forever City started as a single player game, then became episode 1 of the online Shadow World series. The world editor was modified to support the online model of the Shadow Journey games. It was easy to use, supported a rich and powerful editing environment and allowed Shadow Journeys to become the most modded game series of all time. Forever City takes place in a hive city, think Kowloon Walled City on steroids, and follows the path of a kid growing up in the lower sections. Jaime will want to borrow heavily from Forever City for a location in Realms of the Hut. The game was formative for her.

Basic Forever City structure

Utilities

Water

most water is recycled in vast processors in the lower levels
some "new" water is bled in to replace "lost" amounts (evaporation, etc.

Power

combination of systems

solar panels on top of city
core taps under the city
fusion reaction plants ringing the city
methane powered generators run from city waste processing plants

Food

industrious, often poorer, people grow food on the top of their stacks and deep within their stacks

food has to be grown around solar collection equipment
fungus related products are grown in abandoned sections deeper in the city stacks
most is medicinals and specialty items not available from food distribution systems

factory systems outside the city grow raw material
most is shipped in for processing
techs maintain the automated gear that handles the tasks
a few remote farmers deal with animal product production

processing is only what is necessary to send carcasses to city outlets

central government only deals with utilities

food, water, power

Control and Governance

most "control" is by Sectors

Sectors

controlled by person or council, depending on citizenry
"policing" is based on control-freak level of sector governors
many sectors are loose collectives of people, just getting by
collect fees for maintenance of Sector holdings

poorer sectors rely on citizens to maintain their areas

Notes:

think Blade Runner crossed with Kowloon Free City

started high tech and prosperous

diminished over time to mix or mostly poor, some in the middle, and a few with wealth

provides some type of resource to off-world empire, the Ashatari Empire

resources auto-mined
delivered through automated pick-ups
end-game for Forever City segment of Shadow Journeys is to slip aboard a transport and find out where it goes
wealthy few control this resource extraction and sale
minimal infusion of off-world stuff from this system

The Forever City is rather dystopian, with social classes functioning in the following ways:

The Middle Class to Lower Wealth Levels

The more well-off tend to be wage slaves, thinking they are free, but having no freedom at all. Very few of them will take up the adventure that is the core of the game.

The Wealthy

The wealthy have real power, in their sections they control the wage slaves. They in turn are controlled by the 1% who have insane amounts of power and wealth

The POOR

The truly poor are the most free of all the citizens. They live in squallor, tucked into makeshift housing blocks that evolved from the lower class quarters of the original times. They are ignored, as long as they don't venture into the "desirable" areas of the city. They perform the "menial" tasks that keep the city functioning. Many accept the adventure that is the core of the game.
