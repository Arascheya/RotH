Starships - Survival Kit Basics

PERSONAL SURVIVAL KITS

1. Many methods of carrying personal survival kits may be employed. These methods will of course be based on your requirements, individual needs and space in the aircraft. Carrying personal survival kits may pose minor problems, such as filling your flight clothing pockets prior to each flight. These problems should not, however, overshadow the advantages of having such items should the actual need arise.

a. Items may be scattered throughout your flight clothing. This method is effective in that bulk and weight are not a problem. Also the loss of one item doesn't necessarily mean the loss of the entire kit.

b. Individual kits may be packed using small plastic containers, soap dishes, or tobacco tins. Each kit could include like items such as a medical kit, fishing kit, or one kit could be packed to include general survival items.

c. Large survival kits in flight deck and passenger escape modules.

2. A good approach to carrying survival kits is to carry as much as possible keeping in mind supplementing aircraft kits in the most beneficial manner.

a. Minimum essential items:

(1) Knife (preferably pocket type with at least 2 blades).

(2) Matches (should be carried in a waterproof container or dipped in paraffin and in as many places in your clothing as possible).

(3) Fire starters (commercial types, metal match, permanent match or flint and steel).

(4) Needles (sail needles, darning needles).

(5) Snare wire (wire can be wrapped around small containers).

(6) Assorted fish hooks and line (maybe wrapped in aluminum foil).

(7) Bandaids, small dressings, and personal medications.

(8) Insect repellent.

(9) Chapstick.

(10) Water purification tablets (if not available, water must be boiled 2 minutes at sea level and 1 minute in addition for every 1000 feet in altitude).

(11) weapons

(12) communicator/locator

b. Suggested items (as space permits).

(1) Compass.

(2) Small wire cutters.

(3) 6" file.

(4) Sharpening stone.

(5) Pen gun flares and/or smoke flares.

(6) Metal mirror.

(7) Wire saw.

(8) Aluminum foil.

(9) Plastic canteen (prophylactics may be used as water proof containers or canteens).

(10) Safety pins,

(11) Small flashlight w/spare batteries.

(12) Candles.

(13) Razor blades.

(14) Additional fish hooks, line, sinkers, and flies.

(15) Gill net.

c. Medical kits may include:

(1) Bandaids.

(2) Tape.

(3) Small dressings.

(4) Salt tablets.

(5) Iodine.

(6) Iodine tablets (for water purification)

(7) Bacitracin ointment.

(8) Aspirins or APCs.

(9) localization stunners

(10) electronic sutures

(11) vials of universal anti-toxin

d. A variety of spices and food can be carried as space permits. These are a tremendous aid to morale and may be used to improve the flavor of water and small game animals.

(1) salt.

(2) Pepper.

(3) Sugar.

(4) Coffee and/or ten.

(5) Onion and/or garlic flakes,

(6) Dehydrated soups and/or bullion cubes.

(7) Presweetened Kool-aid.
