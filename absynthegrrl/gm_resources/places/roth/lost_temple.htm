Lost Temple

This was a temple erected by the Drkzyrkth to the Keeper of the Balance. This was a splinter group of Drkzyrkth that had embraced religion as well as science. They had begun researches into the past, using a nascent time-probing technology to look deep into their past. Their last experiment sent a time probe back to the beginning and triggered a ripple pulse that anihilated their world. As an act of reverence, the Keeper preserved their temple and linked it to the Great Repository. She allows Repository visitors to visit it as a reminder that the persuit of knowledge can have devastating consequences.

There is a viewing screen on the smaller, outer temple that shows the results of the time probe that caused their destruction.

This is a description of the time probe results from a manuscript by Sri Yugaswar Devi, a plane wanderer of great reknown, from the Kharatoum Sultanate.

We left Centrys for the Sphere of Concordant Discord, through a Door we knew to be safe. Knowledge and safety are an illusory thing as we found when we stepped into a strange realm. Ancient megalithic ruins thrust up from dark, tangled forests. Sharp mountain peaks thrust high into the sky and cast deep shadowy gloom over the narrow valley we were in. Soft footfalls paced through the branches above and beside us. Strange bird-like calls sounded forlorn in the still air. Now anf then a puff of wind would stir the higher branches, rubbling them against each other in strange rythms.

We wandered narrow valleys and thick tangles for days, seeking a Door back to Centrys, or to anywhere else. When we had given up all hope we stumbled across another path, one we had not seen the night before. It wound directly toward one of the towering ruins, climbing a steep stair to a high platform. Below us was one of the valleys, spread out in a canopy of dense brush. A dark doorway yawned behind us. Desperate to leave, we stepped through and onto a narrow strip of dark grey sand.

The dense forest and the valleys were gone. Black void dropped off from both sides of us. Behind us the tongue ended abruptly in nothingness. Ahead was a low cluster of rocks. In front of us a high rock wall faced us. Set into its face was a giant iron door, set between shaply pillars. In an alcove above the door a featureless female statue stared out into the void. Above us was a canopy of stars and a strange swirling pool of light.

The doors would not open to any of our attempts. Irishkah attempted to climb around the statue and encountered a clear barrier just behind it. Within the cavernous room she could see giant statues and a deep pit. Above the pit a glowing red crystal pulsed and shone. She tried to find a lock to pick on the doors but there was none. Ibrahmah attempted to use his arcane skills to open the door, but was unable to as well. We resorted to walking the perimiter of the rock chamber, finding five more doors, all sealed as effectively as the first.

On top of a smaller temple, the only one of the ruins in any sort of shape, we found a strange scrying device. It was of dark metal, pitted with age, yet still whole and solid. As Irishkah approached it, it sprang to life. An image floated upon its surface of a giant surface of fire, pulsing red and angry. Cryptic runes appeared near the bottom of the image as the image entered the fire and passed into a blackness so complete it forced the eye to the firy white sphere seen far in the distance.

The runes read: Time Probe 666 - sent to pre-time through gate one - mission start

The sphere drew swiftly closer and as it came we saw streamers and tendrils rushing out from it, forming into little clouds and these clouds were pulled and sucked into a blackness blacker even the the balck surrounding it. Small motes of light flashed and danced where the deeper black met the firy white.

The cryptic runes flashed again as we saw the little motes swirl together, becomeing a small white light, a small grey blob, and a tiny blacker than black dot. They moved with a sudden swiftness that pulled a gasp from us and violent purple beams lept from them, pinning the large white sphere and the large darkness. The beams twisted and turned, weaving enormous cages of webbing and then a blueish white flash blinded us. When next we could see, only the three smaller spots remained. Small fires sped from the bright one, becoming as stars in the sky, one sped directly at us and when it flared red-orange across the viewer, blackness closed over us.

The second runes read: Indeterminant time shift - mission time - indeterminant - time coords impossible to compute

We awoke in the gardens of the Sultan, to the ministrations of his personal physician and several serving maids.
