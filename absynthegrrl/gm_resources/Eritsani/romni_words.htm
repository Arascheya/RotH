Romni Words

The first section based on andalusian area of spain, the second on british isles

andalusian words

aficianado - flamenco conoisuer, possessing knowledge and deep intuition

alegri`a - gypsy joy, merriment

alegri`as - happy kind of andalusian song and dance

baile - dance

barrio - district/quarter/nieghborhood

bularie - improvised fast gypsy songs and dances, full of gusto, ideal for gypsy juergas

cale`s - spanish gypsies

calo` - language of spanish gypsies

cantaores - folk singers

cante flamenco (chico) - andalusian folk song, light textured, gypsified

cante jondo - deep song, most profound and ancient songs of andalusia

castellano - castillian - as used by gypsies - ‘spaniard’

candro - team of flamenco performers

copla - folk poem or song

cueva - caves

debla - super martinete, ancient song sung without guitar

dichla (la) - luck

duen~ya - proprietress

duende - demon/soul or spirit in folk music

fandongo gitano - song and dance of arabian origin, danced freely by gypsies in granada

feria - fair/holiday

flamenco - flaming/gypsified/lively/andalusian song or dance

gitanari`a - gypsy quarter or district

gitano(a) - spanish gypsy

gracia - grace/cleverness/charm

hado - fate/destiny

hombri`a - manliness

jaleador - beat setter, one who encourages the dancers

juerga - spree/gypsy revelry

kris - gypsy justice/tribunal

malaguen~a - flamenco song of malaga

manto`n - fringed shawl

martinete - song of the forge, cante grande of gypsy metal workers

novio(a) - bridegroom/bride/suitor

olla - gypsy stew/pot of boiled meats and vegetables

patrin (pateran) - gypsy trail signs/secret symbols used to guide and warn nomadic gypsies

payo - non gypsy/frequently denigrating/also busno, gadjo, gorgio, gaje

picaroon - roguish hero, skilled in flattery and persuasion

pitos - snapping of fingers in a gypsy dance

rom - man, husband, gypsy

saeta - an arrow of song, heard during holy week

siguiriya gitana - weeping song, among the oldest and purest of the cante jondo

soleares - songs of solitude, primative archtype of cante jondo

zambra - revelry, night with songs and dances in moonlight
