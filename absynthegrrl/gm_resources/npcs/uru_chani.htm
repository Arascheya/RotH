Chani Kodetova

Current Data

Chani Kodetovais an art historian and fluent linguist. Her primary languages are Tu'ar't'zani, Zeltaaran, and Nasazi. She was the lead on gathering and researching a collection of Nasazi items, held by old tribal members, in Yew Nexico. She was the researcher that the two old tribal elders entrusted the "memory stone" to.They insisted it be taken and kept safe.

She was promoted to director after the recovery of the Nasazi collection. She is still asked to help with difficult linguistic problems. Chani recognizes Shan from several museum of history events where she was there with her mom.

Herbert, her husband, is expert on Zeltaaran art and has some knowledge of Tu'ar't'zani art from the contact between the two groups

Chani Kodetova is an art historian, focusing on the Middle East, and the Director and CEO of the Metropolitan Art Museum of Yew Nork. She is married to Herbert Kodetova, an expert on Zeltaaran history, working at the Natural History Museum of Yew Nork. Herbert, Chani, and Charlotte Stark, President of the Natural History Museum, are very good friends and spend a lot of time together outside of their work.
