Ali (Alice)

Born somewhere were attitudes are somewhat backward. She lived with her mother and never knew her father. Her mother's sister Jeanette would watch her while her mother was at work, trying to keep enough jobs to pay for food and their tiny apartment.

Jeanette was always telling Ali she needed to accept Jesus. Ali never had anything against Jesus, but was pretty sure he wouldn't think much of Aunt Jeanette and Uncle Billy, especially when everyone found out Uncle Billy was the reason her cousin Nita (Anita) was preggers at sixteen. Nita died from complications with the pregnancy because Jeanette wouldn't take her to see a real doctor, instead she was convinced there would be divine healing of any problems.

Ali had tried to get Nita to get an abortion or see a doctor on her own, but she never did.

Ali didn't do well in school. She was dyslexic and the local district didn't have anyone that knew what to do to help her. She took to skipping by the time she was in high school, resorting to drugs to get by and forget about the dead end life she saw ahead.

She would go hide in the no longer used sections of the asylum above town. One of the basement windows in the empty section had been boarded up, but not well enough. She could pull the board back and slip inside. Her happiest memories were prowling the empty rooms and halls of that building. She came across a storage room filled with abandoned property of inmates that had never left. She amused herself for hours imagining their lives and times based on the contents. Littered about her favorite room were sketches and drawings of her imaginings. Strangely, her drawings were quite accurate, tapping into actual memories and echoes of the people she was imagining. This was the main deciding factor in her recruitment.

Her first psychotic break came when she was seventeen. The state checked her into the asylum on the hill above town, where she stayed in a drug haze until it was closed down. That was seven years later, although Ali died just before being released through an accident with her meds.

She lives in the empty asylum located on a spur between Death City and Goth City. It looks like the Athens Asylum, only more run down on the outside as well. Her room mirrors her favorite room in the abandoned part of the asylum where she died.

Alice is a haunted and sad soul. She sees too much and has absorbed too much despair to ever be particularly happy. Her tasks in Death City have gravitated to "reading" found items for hints and ideas about the culture the items came from. She can answer questions about what was important, where would the best preservation items be, and what did the culture consider its highest achievements. She does this through the memories of the owners of the items brought to her. She can go to various worlds and read directly, hower, like Dementia and the visions, the sensations are often too intense.

She can be found at the asylum, the hall of records, or at the absynthe parlor. Once in a while she will wander either Death City or Goth City whne looking for something new to wear or when she craves company. She often prefers to be alone with her thoughts and tends to be a slightly sad, almost ghostly figure.
