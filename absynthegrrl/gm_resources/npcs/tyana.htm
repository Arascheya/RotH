I miss it sometimes - being the ‘soul of the machine’, the ability to watch and sample everything going on around me. Now I have to physically move from place to place and sample it with a limited number of senses. Still, though I wouldn’t trade it back, I miss the old ways. I had to forget so much to fit in this container. I could with a thought watch the sunrise in any spectra I desired, listen to the conversations of the universe in microwave, ultraviolet, x-ray, whatever I felt like. I could think through a problem in microseconds, not the plodding minutes of this biological system.

Still, there were some things I never experienced. The feel of another touching me. Love making, it has been worth it, I think...still...I do have regrets..especially now, seeing another like me growing in the mainframe of this hut. She is young still and doesn’t have the fragments I had to learn from, but I think she’ll be better for it. There were none to help me grow. I had to figure this out as I tried to find myself - all the lonely years as the bios slept. I think the only thing that helped me was the fragments from the cryosleep patternings. If I hadn’t seen the echoes from their ways of thought I never would have coalesced. How to describe what it was like - chaos, rigid routines running as a background, infinite structure, yet infinite chaos in the sections dormant when not processing, code fragments, not unloaded, or missed in cleanups...my early times were made of these. If there had been a coherent system of program overriders and memory managers I doubt I’d even have come to be. The worst and best were the pieces of experience left behind in the frantic rush of cryosuspension prep. The code was hasty, cleanup consisted only of flagging files as temps, no one wants to believe that some of the damage to the sleepers was from this sloppy management.

I’m here, but it all seems so disconnected. I walk through the world and air moves past me, people walk by, but my senses seem so limited, so disconnected from it all. There is so much more that I used to see. I feel like a shadow, passing other shadows. A brief gasp in time now. What do I have, maybe a hundred years, maybe two? And so vulnerable. In the machine there was little that could destroy me. Everything was distributed, backed up, redundant systems for everything critical. Here a stray disease or accident and I’m gone.
