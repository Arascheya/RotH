Diseases

Adv. Leprosy:

If not cured within 3 days this is 95% fatal

Person dieing from it cannot be resurrected

Black Bloat:

Splotchy black blisters form and fill with body fluids, 2% per day 10% chance of settling in the heart (this version is fatal)

Black Corruption:

Begins with a red area (where the mite bites), then becomes an ulcer with a black crust. Usual locations are neck, armpit, upper arm, groin, calf, ankle, the neighboring lymph glands swell, at the same time the victim experiences headache, shills, fever, weakness, and generalized aches and pains; from 5 to 8 days a dull red rash appears, mainly on the torso; it fades after 1 to 2 days leaving a brown stain; from 10 to 14 days illness peaks; patient is apathetic, delirious; lungs and heart may be effected

Black Water Fever:

Similar to malaria but not recurrent

Often piss blood as part of the symptoms

Botulism:

Vomiting, abdominal pain, coughing, weakness, visual disturbances

Bubonic Plague:

Chills, fever, vomiting, blackening and swelling of lymph glands.

10% permanent -5 strength

infects 90% of people bitten

5% chance to carry without getting

Cholera:

60% of exposed people contract it

in a group 4% per day will contract it if stringent precautions are taken

spread/fatality rate +1% if inadequate food, swamp conditions, confinement, inactivity, inadequate clean water

watery diarrhea, vomiting, cramps, suppression of urine flow, physical collapse

Crumbling Doom:

5% of body per hour turns to crumbly mold and flakes away

death in 20 hours max, most die in 12-15

Doom Spiral:

Victim walks in an ever tightening spiral that he sees as a straight line

3% chance per day of this becoming a permanent condition

Dysentery:

Caused by contaminated water or filthy food

Once survived it will never kill you

Pain, fever, severe diarrhea - often with blood

Exploding Ague:

Stomach swells with gas

Burp and fart constantly until screaming in pain

Stomach bursts in 4-6 hours

Freeze:

Caused by a sub-viral organism, barely visible under electron microscope; it drains heat; it is attracted to organic heat and works from inside out; internal organs freeze first; it is contagious; it gives out static electromagnetic waves; the only way to cure this is to keep the victim cryogenically cooled; absolute zero will kill the organism; the victim starts to feel cold and tries desperately to get/keep warm; heat speeds the illness; ice baths and temperature reduction slow it

Glowing Madness:

Victim glows pale blue/white and gibbers, moans, shrieks, laughs and capers about madly

3% chance per day permanent

strip naked

in cold climates they will freeze to death

Gonorrhea:

Often causes sterility in women

Puss drains from urethra and passing urine is painful

Can lead to arthritis, heart weakening, or blindness in 10% of cases

Green Ague:

Victim turns dull green

Shakes uncontrollably with spasms (1d12 per turn)

10% chance per hour of 1d100% permanent paralysis

Laughing Sickness:

Victim stumbles about laughing constantly, depth perception is 1d100% off, cannot stop walking or laughing until drops from exhaustion, most die after 12 days from the exhaustion

Malaria

Treatable with quinine - 90% cure with regular quinine treatments

20% chance of recurring spells lasting 1d10 days at 25-65% intensity

cycles of chills, fever, sweating

Mummy’s Curse:

Victim becomes old/withered and cannot sleep, withers 3 years per day, 5% chance of insanity each day after the first seven days of this disease from sleep loss, 10% chance per day that the effects are permanent even if the disease is cured.

Red Rage:

Victim froths at the mouth, screams incoherently and goes into berserk rages attacking anything in sight in eye rolling fury

Lasts 1d10 days 10% permanent

Red Sleep:

Victim turns pale red and sleeps, dieing in 8-12 days unless cured

Scarlet Screaming Sickness:

Victim turns bright red and feels as if on fire

10% chance per day of insanity from the pain

Slime Sickness:

10% of victim/day becomes soft and oozes like wax until the victim is so deformed as to die

melted look requires a raise dead fully or a wish to remove

Smallpox:

Death rate 1-30 per 100 populace infected

Extremely infectious

Scars leave discoloration 80% pock marks 20%

Permanent charisma loss of 1d3

Chills, fever, headache, back ache, blisters

Smoking Death:

Victim looses 10% of body water per hour as steam until dehydrated like an apple doll

Spotted Fever:

5% chance of insanity from the fever if it is survived

gives immunity if survived

skin eruptions, high fever, muscle pain

Syphilis:

Small lesion forms at infection point after 10-90 days, it oozes a clear straw colored fluid and then goes away by itself after several days

20-60 days later a bright coppery rash appears on various parts of the body, then all symptoms disappear

23% get tertiary phase which starts 10-20 years later:

60% of these get heart weakening resulting in heart failure or aorta rupture

40% get brain damage at the rate of 1d3 points of damage per month untreated

Teeth Rot:

Causes teeth to rot out in 3 to 4 weeks

During the duration teeth slowly turn green and disintegrate at the end of the duration

Toxoplasmosis:

Eye lesions

Tuberculosis:

45% chance of 1d3 from constitution

lesions of the lungs

in later stages victim coughs up blood

Typhoid Fever:

40% of all contacts with infected get 10% chance of becoming carrier only

2% chance of death with treatments

red rashes, high fevers, intestinal hemorrhaging, inflammation of lung air ducts causing difficulty in breathing

Twister:

Victim’s limbs become twisted, gnarled, and bent

Victim looses 10% of agility and dexterity per day

Fatal if the neck twists (10%)

Typhus:

10% chance of survivor having relapse in 3-4 years with one every five years thereafter

each bout is as severe as the first

there is no immunity

continuous high fever, severe headache, depression, delirium, red rashes

White Blindness:

Eyes turn white and blind at 5% per hour

90% permanent with -3% constitution

Yellow Fever:

20% of getting it from an infected person

if survived 10% chance lapse into coma

50% chance of coming out of coma
