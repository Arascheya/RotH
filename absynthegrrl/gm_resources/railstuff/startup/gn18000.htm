How to Boot a Steam Locomotive by Phil Jern



The following short story I originally discovered while browsing the USENET group, "bit.listserv.railroad". I thought it was such a good representation of a nearly lost art (preparing a steam locomotive for a day's work), that I wrote the author, Phil Jern (pjern@indy.net) to ask if I could publish it on my page. He agreed. I hope you enjoy reading, "How to Boot a Steam Locomotive" as much as I did.

Phil Jern (pjern@indy.net) in the cab of ex-Florida East Coast #153, a 4-6-2 "Pacific type" steam locomotive built by Alco in 1922. This is the locomotive Phil was working on when he wrote this story. The 153 is currently stored inoperable awaiting repairs.
