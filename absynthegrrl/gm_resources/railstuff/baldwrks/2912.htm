ATSF Steam Locomotive 2912

ATSF 2912 was one of 30 of these giant locomotives built for the Santa Fe Railway in 1943-44. These locomotives were the largest and heaviest 4-8-4 locomotives ever built in the world! The Pueblo Locomotive & Rail Historical Society, Inc. plans to restore this engine to full operational condition.

Some 2912 Facts.

  • Total weight with tender in working order 961,000 Lbs.
  • Over 79,960 lbs of tractive effort.
  • Overall length 120'10" (only 13 inches shorter than UP's Challenger!)
  • Working boiler pressure 300 PSI., 28"X32" cylinders.
  • Tender Capacity 7000 Gals oil 24,500 Gals water. Tender rides on four axle trucks.
  • 80 Inch Drivers with  all roller bearing axles and side rods.
  • 5,313 Sq.Ft. of heating surface with 2,366 Sq.Ft. of superheater area.
  • The 2912 was built in January of 1944 by the Baldwin Locomotive Works.
