Shelly's Death

Six weeks into our intelligence assignments my father called. "Mirya, you're friend Shelly is in the hospital, in really bad shape. You had best hurry if you want to see her, they say she probably won't last more than a few days."

My commander came by moments after I had hung up. He took one look at my face, asked what had happened, and told me "Get out of here, get your butt to that hospital. If you have someone who can take you, get them to go with you." I mumbled a distracted thanks and hurried to find Annie.

It took only moments to find her and clear her departure with the commander. As we arrived at my car I tossed the keys to her, "You drive." Startled, she looked at me for a moment, then silently climbed in.

At the hospital I hurried to Shelly's room. One look through the door and everything spun for a moment. She was covered mostly in bandages, tubes and wires ran everywhere. When she saw me she managed a shaky smile, "Hi lover."

"What happened?" The tale of her life after leaving for college froze my blood. I couldn't keep the shock and horror off my face as I unthinkingly blurted out, "But WHY?"

"I don't know what happened. Things got a little bad when I told my parents I was lesbian, but I thought they had been coping. Then I was lonely without you and met someone I thought was good for me. She introduced me to some of her friends and things got a little strange, but nothing really raised any flags. Then came a few rather wild parties, and the subtle pressures to try this or that. Somehow I never quite realized what was going on until it was too late. When I tried to get out they did this to me. I wish now that you had been there. You always seemed to know what was going on, and when to back away. How I always envied you. You were always so strong and sure of yourself, always the scrapper. I wish now I had stayed with you."

"Bullshit, I was always the scared, dumb service brat. You were..."

"Stop it, just STOP IT! If you love me at all you will stop doing that to yourself. You are stronger than you know, the rest of us see it. Why can't you?" Slowly the room came in to focus as we talked of happier times. There were no flowers or cards, nothing but empty walls and a small table with a pitcher of water and a deck of playing cards. The other bed was empty. I wanted to ask where her family was, if they even knew what happened, but somehow I didn't have the courage. Time passed quickly and slowly, a feeling I still don't understand. All too soon a nurse was telling us only a few more minutes until visiting time was over. "Would you please hold me? For a few moments, like you used to?"

Carefully I threaded my way through the tubes and wires and held her, my cheek resting against hers. "Remember, I love you," was the last thing she said. I couldn't see through my tears as suddenly alarms went off all over the place. It took long moments before I realized what they really meant. I screamed "No, Shelly!" just as the trauma team arrived. I was shoved aside and left slumped in a corner while they worked on her. Even I could see it wasn't helping. Another nurse charged into the room with yet another piece of equipment and stopped cold when she saw me. She dropped her burden and then gently lead me from the room.

The next thing I remember with any clarity was Annie's arms around me, as I cried myself out. Just when I thought I was done a young doctor entered the room. He took in the scene, blushed, and then nervously began, "Ah.. I'm afraid Ms. Kingston is gone. We tried everything we could, but it didn't help. I'm terribly sorry." He continued for a few more minutes, then started to retreat.

"Has her family been notified?"

He turned even redder as he answered, "The people she gave as her parents say they have no daughter by that name." If he could have he would have run from the room, as it was he just hurried. When the shock wore off all I saw was red as I stormed to the nurse's station and demanded a phone. I was half way through dialing when Annie finally got through the rage with her calm words. "I know you want to kill them, but what about your friend, shouldn't something be done for her?"

I stopped and thought a bit. She was right as usual. "What arrangements are made for someone without next of kin?"

"Usually they are cremated and buried after a simple non-denominational service, why?"

"I want to make the arrangements for her burial. Is there any way I can do that?"

"I'm sure something can be worked out. You'll need to check with the business office and her doctor though."

The arrangements took several days and cost me a great deal of my savings, but when the memorial was finished and her ashes given to me, I felt better able to accept what had happened. Rather than vent my anger at her parents I sent them an invitation to the service. They never even acknowledged the note. I used the gym facilities more strenuously over the next months until I had vented most of the anger I still felt towards them.
