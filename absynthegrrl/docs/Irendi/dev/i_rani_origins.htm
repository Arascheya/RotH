When her team was out near Jhomolhari they took refuge in the fortress ruins near it. During the night she couldn’t sleep and went wondering through it. In the lowest open section was a segment of wall that looked less permanent than the rest. Her attention was drawn to a very faint painting of a man on a flying tiger and a seven point star underneath. Near it was the representation of a mountain looking much like Jhomolhari and a door set into a smaller mound drawn near it - very much like the small hill the ruins sat on. Carefully she rubbed away more dirt and debris until she could seen the entire scrap of painting. The bricks had shifted causing cracks to appear in the plaster beneath the drawing. One set of cracks framed a single brick and the drawing of the door on it. The way the door exactly matched the brick beneath, including a small warp in its top, sparked her curiosity. Without really thinking about what she was doing, she pulled out her pocket knife and used it to gently pry the brick from the wall.

Behind it was the rough stone of the hill and a small dab of plaster. It seemed to fill a tiny spot in the otherwise uniform stone. It was quick work with her knife to pry it loose and reveal a small sculpted indent in the stone. An indent that seemed to mirror the odd rounded seven facetted stone of her new ring.
