Soon after the smell of cooking meat mingled with the smell of burning pine.

I think she was going to tell me more, but her father called and she spooked, running from the shop as if demons were on her tail." For a few minutes they talked about how strange it was, but the fire, the slowly sinking sun, and the faint breeze, gently stirring the fall leaves, soon took their minds off it.

When Dan went to grab a few more chunks of wood, Lara kicked together a pile of fallen leaves and collapsed back into them. "This is my favorite time of year. Fresh leaves, warm nights, and a clear sky with the promise of stars," she said as she lay back.

For long moments there was silence. Shae and Roxy curled up with each other near the fire, Dan setting a couple more wood pieces on the fire, and Lara staring up at the sky. "Why do we chase so much crazy stuff as people?" Lara asked from her leaf pile. Eventually, she stood and moved trance-like into the forest, picking a path through the undergrowth toward a steep part of the hill-side. Dan followed her when she wouldn't answer his calls, Shae and Roxy trailing after. They followed her to a low cliff face and watched as she made her way along the scree at its base to a point at the most vertical part of the rock. She walked up to the face, turned sideways and slipped through the rock. The others stopped in shock, unable to believe what they had seen.

Roxy was the first to shake off the paralysis. She slowly picked her way to that place where Lara had last been, turned like Lara had, and edged sideways slowly. Instead of bumping into the expected rock, she felt a hint of cold, then saw a flat rock wall in front of her eyes. A narrow passage extended to her left, and another rock wall was at her back. Etched into the rock were various pictographs and odd runes, inlaid with an red-orange polished stone. Pulsing bluish white light came from the passage to her left.

Roxy started down the tunnel as Dan and Shae stepped through the concealed opening. They emerged to find Lara staring into a glowing ball of swirling and pulsing light, shifting across the color spectrum and roiling like boiling water. Directly between the entrance and the bubble was a short granite pillar with a black alabaster statue standing on it. The figurine was of a woman with unfurled bat wings and arms stretched out from her sides. Her hands pointed toward similar figurines one each side of her. Seven figurines were arranged in a ring around the pulsing bubble. Each column was placed in the center of a seven pointed star, formed of odd shaped runes inlaid in hematite.

Lara walked slowly to the nearest figurine, reached out, and set her hand on it. A bubble of red fog sprung up around the shimmering inner bubble and faint lines of red beamed between their hands. Lara woke late in the night, coming wide awake in the moon lit tent. She lay for a while, litstening to Dan breathing next to her and staring at the top of the tent. Sleep did not return. Instead, something called to her, faintly, from the forest.

Eventually she slipped out of the sleeping bag, carefully, so as not to wake Dan. She slipped on a long white T-shirt and her underwear and stepped out into the night. Outside a full moon hung high in the sky. The wind had stopped and everything was still and silvery.

The call came fromdeeper in the trees, down the hill, toward the strange cave they had found earlier. Lara hesitated only a moment before following the call. She slid into the cave and walked directly to the glowing red field and placed a hand on it. Words formed in her mind and an opening appeared. She walked through and stepped into the energy bubble within.

She stepped out into a small, bubble room, with a narrow passage directly in front of her. The walls were swirling green and black fog, solid to the touch, but neither warm, nor cold. The patterns were almost hypnotic, drawing her in, leaving her standing in place for what could have been seconds or years, she couldn't tell which.

Finally she shook her head and broke free. Behind her was a shimmering distortion in the wall, where she had stepped through. Instead of returning, she walked down the narrow passage, which emerged into a large room with seven additional passages leading out of it. Six were the same narrow tubes that she had emerged from. The seventh was larger and went only a short way until it entered the side of another tunnel which stretched off to the left and right.

Lara walked to the tunnel and stared off intothe distance of it. There was nothing in sight except empty tunnel stretching away. She walked to the wall across it and touched it. At first it felt solid, but as she moved her hand around it, feeling the texture, it softened and split open in an iris shaped opening. Stepping through, she found herself in a featureless fog of green and black. Just ahead of her was a set of seven descending passages, clustered in a circle.

A brighter green glow emanated from the tube where the iris had closed behind her and a similar glow pulsed on the section of ground between the seven passages down. Her curiosity led her deeper into the fog. The green glows remained behind her, waiting to guide her back. A silent wind lifted and played in her hair. Likethe walls, it neither warmed her nor chilled her. The further she ventured into the fog, the more at peace she felt, as if she had come home to a safe place. Stress dropped from her like a weight and she spun in place, arms outstretched, glorying in the utter relaxation, atlastsinking to the soft ground and laying back to stare into the patterns of the fog.

Slowly a nagging thought intruded into her peace, a sense she was forgetting something. It drifted closer, reminding her of friends left in a different place. Reluctantly she climbed to her feet and looked around, afraid she was lost. The green glows were beacons that led her back to the strange tunnel and the iris wall.

Her phone showed only two hours had passed fromthe time she stepped fromthe tent until she returned to it. She slid back into bed next to Dan and snuggled close, immediately falling back to sleep.
