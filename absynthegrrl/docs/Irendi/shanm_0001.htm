Tearing the Veil

Something bit me two weeks before my first term at Davisson School, a world reknown dance, music and theatre school, located in the Jefferson Centre complex. I was at work, at the organic grocery, and had my hands deep in a bin of produce. A bee buzzed up just after the pain, I didn't think anything about it since I had been stung numerous times before, as I smashed it.

Twenty minutes later waves of heat rolled over me and sweat popped out, running down my face and soaking my clothes. Ms. Schultz, dressed in her typical shapeless, flower patterned mumu, stared and said, "You don't look very good, dear. What's the matter?"

"I don't know," I replied as a wave of dizziness hit. I stumbled into the bakery case I was getting her bread from. She came around, grabbed my arm, and steadied me as I almost fell.

"Let me help you. Mr. Arakov, could you help us to the back?" she helped me walk toward the doors to the back. I was starting to shake by the time we reached the doors. Mr. Arakov, the owner, came in behind us, just as violent cramps doubled me up and dropped me to the cold concrete floor. I fell into blackness as the convulsions started.

My ears woke up first to the sounds of a heart monitor, voices in the distance, and paging speakers. Next came smell, disinfectant and cheap food. Vision returned last, blurry and foggy, like waking up with pink eye, where your lids are mostly crusted shut. I tried to reach them with my left hand, but it wouldn't move. My right hand wouldn't move either and panic started to set in. Something was irritating my nose, making it itch and there was a clogged feeling in my throat.

A Korean looking woman, in a white coat, came into view as nervousness started to increase into panic. "Ah, you're awake. Please, hold still a minute and let me get the feeding tube out." She carefully pulled a long tube out of my nose and then she untied my right hand. She waited while I scraped away most of the clots and opened my eyes. They didn't track at first. If you remember the really old television sets, the analog ones, they would turn "snowy" and wavery when a station wasn't coming in, that was what my eyes were doing. After much blinking and squinting, I looked at the doctor.

"I'm Dr. Kim and I have been attending you. Do you know who you are?" I nodded and managed to whisper my name, through my very dry throat. "Thirsty?" I gave a small nod and she waved at a nurse. A moment later she tipped a few ice chips into my mouth from a small cup. "Do you know what happened?"

"Not really, no. I was working with the produce, same kinds as always, I got stung by a bee, but I've been stung before without reacting, then I went over to sell some bakery to a regular..."

"Stung? We found a bite on your left hand, but it wasn't a sting, there was no stinger. There was a bit of irritation, but none of the usual spider toxins in the tissues. What sort of allergies run in your family?"

"I don't know. I was adopted when I was a baby and never knew my birth parents. How long have I been here?" I had lost track of how many times I had to explain why my parents looked nothing like me. They were average Meriksans, a little thick with age, but they both turned heads. My features were pure Nehtanese, slightly up-turned almond eyes, long, thin face with very high cheek bones, narrow chin, and a wide mouth. Like everyone, I didn't believe I was beautiful, but I was exotic enough to turn heads and had to turn away the boys that came panting after me. I wasn't comfortable with their attention, it distracted from focusing on dancing and was vaguely disturbing as well.

"Four days."

"Four days? When can I go home? I have to be at Davisson School on Sunday to check in and start new student orientation and I'm not ready!" I struggled to sit up, but my vision snowed out and dizziness forced me back down. "Ok, not today, I guess." I looked around the room at the wires and tubes attached to me and tried to stay calm. "Any chance some of these attachments can come off?"

"Some of the monitors can come off. The IV needs to stay in a while longer, until I'm sure you can eat and drink normally. I want to monitor you tonight, make sure you can eat and drink without problems, then move you to a normal room tomorrow. If you are doing well by tomorrow night, I will consider releasing you. I'll check in on you in the morning."

"Where are my parents? In the movies, they are sitting with me while I'm in a coma."

"They have been allowed to see you, but not to stay. You are in Trauma 3, a special ward for the more exotic cases."

"What's wrong with me?"

She hesitated for a long while before speaking, "It's complicated, not because I don't know what happened, but the explanation is going to sound very strange. You're only the second case of this I've seen. A spider bit you, but it wasn't toxic in the usual way. It carries a venom that awakens its victim, strips away the mind's ability to ignore things that don't fit normal perceptions. In one in ten thousand cases a viral contaminant causes a genetic rewrite of the victim. You fell into that percentage and the rewrite caused your convulsions and extreme fever. The conversion runs between two and eight days. The results are varied, however, some things are consistent. That's the easy part." Dr. Kim stopped again.

"What's the hard part? Will I turn into a wolf with the full moon?" I asked.

"No, therianthropy isn't transmitted by spiders, or mosquitos either, thankfully. Do you like science fiction or fantasy?"

"Urban fantasy more than science fiction, but I read and watch both. Why?"

"Then you understand the idea of being able to see things others can't or won't, right?" I nodded. "The rewrite has awakened you to that state. This world contains gates that lead to other places. Doorways to other-wheres that awakened, or aware, beings can use to travel. It is these travelers that you will detect. The dangerous ones will be here to do mischief and they will resent your trying to prevent that. There is a lot more to explain, but the best people to do it are the women running Ezra's Magic Shop. They know a lot more about how this works than I do. Once you are released, I seriously recommend you talk to them. I also recommend you get training in how to fight."

"What if I don't want it? I mean it's cool and all, just I want to be a dancer, not some shadow fighter. Leave it to people who enjoy that kind of conflict. Every story I know states there is a high price for this kind of ability. What if I'd rather not pay it?"

"The price is your transformation. The choice is always yours, however, don't be surprised if your choice isn't respected. The transformation is not reversible. I seriously recommend talking to the people at Ezra's. Do you know where it is?"

"Near the original Arakov Market, in the Village, right?" Dr. Kim nodded. "I'll think about it. Where are my parents? When can I go home?"

"I'm going to keep you overnight. In the morning we will take you to a room in the main hospital and let your parents in to collect you. Now, I'd like you to sleep, so I'm putting you back under for the night. You shouldn't have as much trouble waking up in the morning."


I drifted awake in another room. My eyes opened to grey shadows. Watery light, tinted just the faintest of yellow spilled into the room. Everywhere the shadows were deep and rich, layered into multiple shades of grey and black. Dim red eyes flickered in them. My mom and dad were sitting next to the bed, as grey as the rest of the surroundings. Dr. Kim was telling them I should be awake in moments. She was in full color. Panic settled in as the drugs faded further. Dr. Kim rushed everyone out when I struggled to speak, unable to get much more out than, "Wha, wha's happening? Oh gods!"

"What's wrong? Are you ok?"

"They're grey. Why is the room grey? Why are my parents grey? Why are you in color? What the hell is going on?"

"I told you that you became a Strange Attractor, right?"

"A Strange what?"

"A Strange Attractor, that's what the awakened call those like you. Each Strange gets different abilities and effects. In your case the detecting awakened by color is one of yours. There may be other effects that manifest later. The only consistent side effect is better healing. Trauma 3 was set up for Awakened. The rest of the hospital wasn't. Obviously I am an awakened. Your parents wouldn't be. Can I let them back in? You scared them pretty badly."

"Not yet. I can't go around color blind. Can you do anything about this?"

Not really, no. Maybe they have something at Ezra's, but there's nothing I can do medically to fix you. I wouldn't tell your parents."

"What?"

"Don't tell you parents what's going on, not even the color blindness, not until you talk to the Bishop sisters at Ezra's. They might be able to give you something so you can see, and that would be hard to explain. As for the rest, you might Awaken them, but more likely they'd think you suffered brain damage from your illness."

"Fuck, ok. Let them in," I said with a heavy sigh.

She went to the door, opened it, and said, "She should be ok now. Just a bad reaction to coming out from under the meds we've been giving her."

My mom slowly approached, leaned in, and carefully hugged me around all the attachments. My mother and father took turns telling me what had been happening during the last few days.

"Have you been taking care of yourselves?"

"We can always take care of ourselves. It's you we've been worried about!"

"I'm in good hands here. I know you want to be here for me and I appreciate it, but it's ok for you to take care of yourself as well. You don't have to stay here for me all the time. I'm not that good of company anyway, being tired, sore, and uncomfortable."

My dad turned to Dr. Kim and asked, "When can we take her home?"

"Tomorrow. I would like one more night to make sure the meds don't cause any more trouble and to make sure there aren't any unexpected after effects of her illness."

"Talia would like to see you, when you are up for it," mom added. She hesitated before saying, "You know that girl has a serious crush on you, right?"

"Talia? You're kidding, right?"

"No, very serious. She only leaves the waiting area for work, or when we kick her out to get some sleep. You should be careful not to hurt her."

"She told you?"

"Not directly, but the way she was asking about you, the shattered but hopeful look on her face in the waiting areas, and that she is here every moment she can be, told me. I know your thoughts on relationships right now, but try to be kind, I really like her. Should I let her know you'd be ok seeing her?"

"Yes, you can."

Mom dialed her phone and talked a few minutes before saying, "She'll be here this afternoon, after her shift."

"You could take off this afternoon, let Talia keep me company for a little bit, then they'll probably put me to sleep early this evening anyway."

"Are you sure? We're worried about you, you know."

"I know, and I love you and dad for it. I'll be ok today and I'll see you in the morning, ok?"

She nodded slowly and went to the door. Just after they took away my lunch tray Talia bounded in, "Hey, how are you doing? You going to be ok?"

"I'm..." my voice trailed off as I saw her. She was in full color, but the face I knew was flickering and fuzzing, like a scifi movie bad holographic effect over the top of another face. She was average height, with light blonde hair, and freckle covered pale skin. She would have been pretty if she would dress in things other than ratty hand me downs from her brothers, would take an interest in the state of her hair, and would lose the thick rimmed black plastic glasses. I had tried a few times to talk to her about it, being no stranger to the usefulness of exotic beauty myself, but she had no interest. Slowly the face I knew faded into a different face, darker, more angular, with bone white hair and small horns poking up through it, emerald eyes with cat pupils instead of round ones. Her teeth were sharper and whiter, her cheek bones higher and more pronounced, and she was a lot more muscular than I had thought.

"Shan, are you ok? Why are you staring at me?" Talia asked.

"I'm just so happy to see you," I replied quickly, unsure what else to say. Her face lit up with a wide smile as she replied, "I'm very glad you are ok. I don't like hospitals or seeing my l...," it was Talia's turn to stumble over her words, "friends in them."

We talked for a the rest of the afternoon and through dinner, then Dr. Kim came in. "Ms. Mason needs her rest. You can come back in the morning when her parents come to take her home."


I woke slowly to Dr. Kim watching from near the bed. "How are you this morning?"

"Still seeing in black and white, but otherwise ok, I think."

"Try slowly standing and let me know if there is dizziness, nausea, or extreme weakness. You'll be a bit unsteady from being in bed for four days, but you should be able to stand and walk slowly." I eased myself out of bed, gripped the rail for a moment as I waited for my balance to settle, then slowly walked around the room.

"Things seem ok," I replied after a few minutes.

"Good! I'll let your parents know they can get you after lunch."


When my parents came, I was wheeled to a cab. I had wanted to walk, but the nurses insisted I use the wheel chair. The ride home was difficult, trying to pretend nothing was wrong while looking at a grey world.

Dad ordered in my favorite foods for dinner and we sat and talked about what had happened. I could tell they were frustrated that I didn't know more. Mom would tear up when she thought I wasn't looking. After a long silence my dad asked if I was going to keep working at the market near our place. When I said yes, he got even more quiet. "Look, I could have been bitten anywhere. It wasn't the Arakov's fault. I was planning to see him tomorrow and see when I could start up again. I want to contribute to my school costs," I told him.

"I understand that, but we aren't so poor that we can't fund school. We want you to focus on learning, so you can get the best possible jobs when you are done. Besides, didn't the orientation letters tell you to avoid commitments outside Davisson and focus on your studies? Everyone agrees you could easily be a lead dancer almost anywhere, even now. If you pick up some business schooling on the side, you can run your own company or dance related business if anything happens to you."

"I just don't want to feel like a parasite and I like Talia's family and helping them out. I'll let him know about the school recommendations."

"Please, just be careful," my mom added. "You have no idea how hard it is to see you lying on a bed, in an isolation chamber, wondering if you are going to live or die. I don't want to see that again."

The evening was long and awkward, with everyone heading off to bed as soon as was possible.


The next morning they hovered around me as they got ready to go off to their days. I suffered through it, acting as cheerful as I could, until they finally left. Once they were gone I threw on clothes and headed for the subway. Reading the signs and arrows was much harder without the color coding that made the process easy. Nothing was too strange until I started walking toward the market from the W 4 St. station. All the buildings on the east side of 6th were in color, everything to the west wasn't. As I turned the corner onto Waverly the color hit me like a wave. Everything was in hyper-color, over-saturated, like a photograph were the color is pushed hard. It beat on my eyes until I had a roaring headache by the time I was in front of Ezra's Magic Shop.

The windows were lined with magic kits, simple tricks, and novelties. Inside the front area was cluttered with shelves of more. The further into the shop I went the more esoteric things became. The easy kid's party tricks were replaced with books on magic, rolled up blue prints for old trick apparatus that were no longer considered secret. At the very back I thought I had stepped into an old style apothecary shop. Bottles of powders, potions, and indescribable things cluttered high shelves. A worn counter sat on a glass case filled with various knives, mortars, pestles, and other herbalist gear. As I was trying to read some of the labels, the bead curtain between two tall shelves parted and a woman dressed in movie gypsy clothes walked in. She was my height with black hair, dark brown eyes, and an Eastern European face. "Oh, hello. I thought someone came in. How may I help you?" she asked.

"Um, hi, um, I, uh, Dr. Kim says hi," I stammered out.

"Would you be Kilashandra Mason, then? I'm Olivia Bishop, one of the owners. Dr. Kim said you might stop by. Let me make sure we won't be disturbed, then we can go upstairs and talk." She hurried to the front of the store, then came back.

"What about customers?"

"The ones we care about will come again, the others, well, there aren't that many anyway." She motioned me to precede her through the curtain. It led into a standard stock room, with a stairway up the left side. Upstairs was a richly appointed sitting room, done in movie style gypsy colors and patterns. Light streamed in through the large front windows. "Please, sit. Now what can I do for you?"

I settled slowly into the large armchair. It enfolded me perfectly, the most comfortable chair I had ever sat in. "Can you tell me what is going on? What is a Strange Attractor? Can you tell me why I see most of the world in grey scale? Do you have something for a raging headache as well? Your street is in hyper-color and it's too much."

"The grey scale is easy. Awakening changes everyone in some way. I think we have something that can help with that. There'll still be a difference between multi-versal things and single realm ones, but it should bring most of it back. Give me a moment to get a couple of possibilities." She went back down the stairs, returning several minutes later with a hand full of various items. "Ok, hold each of these and see which one works best. You want something that lets you concentrate easily to turn off the effect, but that knocks it back enough that you can function and that restores most of the color. It's ok if you want to go back a block, outside this enclave of awakened. Keep them all in this silk cloth and only touch the one you are testing."

I returned a few minutes later, after testing all of the items, pointed at two, and said, "The first one gives the best color. The second one is the easiest to switch with, but the color isn't as good. Problem is, how do I wear any of these while dancing? I start the Davisson dance program this Sunday."

"I can make you ear studs that combine the best properties of your two picks. They should be ready by the end of the week."

"How much will I owe you?"

"We can work out something you can afford. Now, on to multi-verse 101, or how the true world works. First, some terms. A multi-verse is the collection of all things that comprise current creation. Within the multi-verse are worlds. Worlds are places that contain various races, beings, and circumstances. Each world contains one or more realms. Realms are single instances of beings and things. For example, you are a single instance of all possible Kilashandra Mason's in all the realms forming the world you call "Earth". There are ways to travel through the multi-verse. This realm started to explore space, that is a simple travel method, but a valid one. It takes longer and is more limited than other ways. Hyperspace or warp or whatever you want to call faster than light travel is better, but still limited in how many realms can be touched. Then there are Gates. Gates come in two basic styles, World Gates, and Web Gates. Gates don't open onto every realm either, but they can be artificially created on an as-needed basis using basic research and powerful technology or magic. Then there are various magical constructs and artifacts, as well as some beings with natural travel powers. The reality is even more complicated than this simple explanation, but this gets the basics across. Does it make sense so far?"

"Maybe, I don't know. Seems a bit like an urban fantasy novel or some new-agey pablum more than reality."

Olivia laughed. "New age has nothing to do with reality, any more than any belief system. Beliefs are about how you want things to be, whether it is real or not."

"Did Dr. Kim mention anything about me?"

"You mean that you are a Strange Attractor? Yes, she did. Stranges deal with Gate Leak, for want of a better term. There is a group that goes from realm to realm securing the permanent gates to keep the worst things out, but the seals aren't perfect. The only way to completely seal a permanent gate is to destroy it, and that is a very difficult task indeed. As a Strange Attractor you are attuned to the gates of your realm. When nasty things come through, you work to suppress them. You will be drawn to them and they will be drawn to you. My sister, Mira, and I can help you with any magic you might have acquired through your change, however, I would strongly recommend you talk to Martin Esterhaaz about fighting training as well."

"Magic I might have picked up?"

She must have seen the skepticism in my eyes as she replied, "Yes, magic. Not illusion like the kits we sell at the front of the store, but real magic. Real magic is like any other technology. It is a way of manipulating matter and energy to get desired results. It can be studied, learned, and expanded upon, just like any other technology. Most Stranges have magic abilities in one form or another. We can help you determine what form yours takes."

"Hold on, I don't want to fight, or learn magic, or anything else. I just want to go back to my life as a dancer."

"I'm afraid it isn't that simple. Once you are awakened, there's no 'un-awakening' possible. I respect your desire not to fight, but the multi-verse may not give you a choice. I will, however. Just remember, we are here in case you need us. This was a lot to take in. Do you have any questions?" she asked as she handed me a card. "This is my private number. Call or stop in when you have more questions. Would you like to borrow these two items until the end of the week? I know what their spells are, so I can make your studs without needing them."

"Yes, I would. May I stay here for a bit and see if the headache will calm down?" She nodded and I remained for another half hour. We talked about random things off and on between pleasant silences.

During one of them, I used my phone to check the work schedules at the nearby Arakov Market. I wasn't sure what I was going to say to Talia after the hospital stay and wanted to be sure she was working at another store. Satisfied, I finally stood and made my exit.

Out on the street the colors were back to almost normal as I strode the short distance to the market. Mr. Arakov understood when I explained about school and schedules, reassuring me that I could always return to work when things settled down. He also apologized profusely for what happened. I kept reassuring him that he wasn't at fault. If he had one weakness, it was that he tended to be nice, even when a bit of hardness would help.

I made it home later than I had planned, but still long before my parents did.


School started in a whirlwind right after dorm check-in. Orientation was intense immersion and classes continued the pressure. The semester flew by in a haze of dance, academics, and student projects. Dorm life was difficult at first. I had never lived with other people so closely before. There were many things I had to adjust to, from the periodic wait for one of our bathrooms, to cleaning chores, to how not to trip over the others. It would have been harder if each of us hadn't been so busy. Many nights it was return to the rooms and fall into bed.

Each dorm suite had five bed rooms, two and a half baths, and a common area. Three were double rooms and two were singles, for returning upper class students. Marta, my bedroom mate, and I had been corresponding through the summer, ever since our housing assignments. It made moving in with her easier. She was a nervous sort, with half of her questions being about what Yew Nork was like, if there was a lt of crime, and other uneasy questions. She was a drama student, and that summed up her first days. I found her sticking close to me for every part of orientation, except when we had to be with our own division. I think she would have dropped out right away, if it hadn't been for the calm of the orientation leader and my efforts.

Marta was thin, to the point of waifish, with wide eyes, in a long face,framed with dishwater blonde hair. I liked her, even as she shadowed me. Her confidence increased a bit once classes began and more structure was introduced to her life. She didn't see it, but she was an excellent actress, scoring the covetted role of the nurse in Romeo and Juliet.

Marta talked me into participating in Romeo and Juliet, as one of the crowd and dancers at the party. It was fun, although, the drama between actors was even worse than the drama between dancers. Marta was adept at slipping past the ego wars, although she couldn't see it.

It was mid October and we had finished the last performance of Romeo and Juliet. Marta and I were heading down my shortcut out of the theatre. I would leave through the theatre loading dock, walk down the driveway, and to the street beyond. From there, it was easy to slip into the side entrance to the Tyler building and up to our dorm room. I had missed Marta in the dressing rooms, so was 20 meters behind her when a strange patch of darkness caught my eye. It was forming almost under the light mid way down the drive, coalescing into a solid figure, appearing slightly behind her. I didn't think about it, just ran after Marta, slamming into the figure as it was about to reach out and grab her. "Run!" I yelled as the figure spun at me, stabbing its hand into my side. Pain exploded and I staggered back.

It shoved me down, leaned over, and hissed, "I will taste your sweetness later, my pretty." What I saw up close was terrifying. It was impossibly thin, tall, with a sharp twisted face. Long claws protruded from stubby fingers. A long tongue snaked out, tasting the air, like a lizard or snake. Its eyes were flat black and dead looking and its breath smelled like rotting blood.

"Shan," Marta called weakly, turning and starting back toward me.

"Run Marta, run!" I screamed at her, although pain turned it into a hissing whisper instead. The figure rose, turned, and started toward her. I reached out and locked my hands on its legs. The claws slashed down, carving tracks across the right side of my face. Blood half blinded me and my grip started to slip. It twisted and got loose. Marta was running for the street and the terror was turning to give chase. "No!" I hissed as I pulled my legs under me and shoved myself up and at the thing. It started to spin back, sinking its claws in my side again. I shoved my hands against its chest and hissed "No!" again. Pain, rage, and terror fueled a white hot explosion in my hands. I saw magenta streamers sizzle through its chest, vaporizing a trail across it. The body fell in two pieces and fizzed away into thin oily smoke. I dropped to my knees and then face down into the alley.


Once more I woke to hospital noises and smells. I opened my eyes and pawed at them, trying to get them to clear. Bandages around my right eye interfered with much of the view. My mom was sitting at my bedside again, pale and washed out with worry. I wasn't really surprised when Dr. Kim walked in a few minutes later.

"What were you thinking, running into trouble like that?" my mom asked.

"I couldn't stand back and watch. There wouldn't have been time to get security."

She half smiled, half grimaced and shook her head. "Do you have any idea how fortunate you were? Just a fraction of an inch and they would have been frantically trying to fix your insides. As it is, there will likely be scars from the ragged knife wounds you had."

My eyes widened in surprise and I started to say something until Dr. Kim barely shook her head. "I know, but I couldn't let it, him, get to Marta. Did they catch him?"

"No, no they didn't," my mom replied.

"There's a police detective who would like to speak with you when you are ready. She would like to get a statement while events are fresh in your memory. Your dorm mates are out there, too, waiting for word. They can come in, one at a time, if you and your parents agree," Dr. Kim added.

"Sure, I can talk to the detective, if you think I'm not too drugged up. I'd like to see my friends after the detective."

"Do you feel coherent? If so, you're probably ok," Dr. Kim replied. She poked her head out the door and motioned to someone.

"Hello, I'm Detective Bishop." She stopped and looked at my mom a moment, "Ms.Mason, how are you? I haven't seen you since the museum problem a few years ago."

"I'm fine, Detective Bishop. All's quiet at the museum these days, thanks. I know you will get to the bottom of this attack on my daughter."

"May I have a few moments alone with her to go over what happened?" She was tall and slender, with dark black hair, dark eyes, and a faint accent, making her seem very familiar. My mom and Dr. Kim looked at me. I shrugged and nodded. Once they had stepped out of the room, Detective Bishop pulled a small recorder from her pocket and asked, "Mind if I record this?"

"You can record us," I replied.

She switched it on, placed it on the small table near my bed, and said, "It's not really a recorder. It's a sound damper, in case someone outside gets too curious." The detective pulled out a sketch pad and flipped it open to a page, then turned it so I could see it. "Is this what attacked you?" On the pad was a sketch almost perfectly mirroring the tall, thin creature that had attacked me.

"How did you know?"

"There have been witnesses to previous attacks and the damage done to you matches that done to the other victims. What did you do to it?"

"Why do you think I did something?"

"If you hadn't, you and Ms. Coseco would be in the morgue."

"How is Marta?"

"Physically, she is fine. Otherwise, she's very confused, at least that's what we've let her believe. Her mind doesn't want to accept what she saw. Dr. Kim's team have been subtly suggesting she doesn't remember well from the trauma. I think they are going to release her today."

"I was more seriously injured than I am feeling right now."

"You will have to ask Dr. Kim about that. Now, what did you do to it?"

"What's going on here? Who are you, really?"

"I am Detective Nikki Bishop with YNPD," she fished her badge out and showed it to me. "I am also Olivia Bishop's sister. She told me there was a new Strange in town. I was going to contact you, but she said to leave you alone, something about choices you wanted to make. Can you tell me what you did?"

"I dissipated it, somehow. Something I did caused it to dissolve into black vapor and drift away."

"How?"

"I don't know. It was attacking Marta and I was fading out. I screamed at it, grabbed it, shoved at it and somewhere during that violet light flashed from my hands and, and, vaporized its chest. Oh gods, I killed it. I killed something." I stared at my hands, for a moment seeing it happening in my memory.

"You actually might have. Congratulations, those things are very hard to put down. The thing you killed was a Yelgezeth. It is a feeder for a Belgezyeleth, a nasty fear sucking over-demon. I won't burden you with details just now, since we shouldn't take a lot of time. You can come to One Police Plaza and talk to me if you'd like more information. Ask for me and someone will show you to my office. Here is my card with my private number on the back. Call me any time. You can also call my sisters, their numbers are on the card as well. Don't try to do this alone. You aren't invulnerable, or a super being, even as a Strange."

"Detective," she paused, her finger almost on the button, "are there a lot of awakened? Is there a secret handshake or anything when I see someone that is?"

"There's never been a census done. I know there are quite a few, in pockets, in the city. Probably more scattered around. There's no club or anything.
