Investigations

Irena dragged herself up the stairs from Waverly's. It had been a long day, starting with the early morning shift and covering through the mid evening change. The thudding bass of the industrial band followed her up the stairs, but reduced drastically once she was in the apartment and had swung the door closed. Once in a while it bothered her enough to consider moving, but the rent was ridiculously low because she managed the bar for Thomas Waverly O'Malley. She dropped the container of food from Waverly's kitchen on the dining counter, stepped into her cluttered bedroom and fell backward onto the bed, staring at the ceiling, and trying to wind down from the hassles of the day.

A desire for food drew her to her feet, where she stripped off her work attire of tall lace up black boots, fabric corset top, and long black peasant skirt. She slipped into a large men's button up shirt and fastened half the buttons. After a few minutes of stretching and massaging her feet, she headed for the kitchen and the counter between it and the living area where she had set her food. The daily special, in this case Indian curry, slid down easily. The latest cook was a god send, having breathed new life into the menus of Waverly's and the sister bar, O'Malley's. Grabbing her laptop, she sprawled back in the couch, loaded it up, and scanned rapidly through her email.

Irena was deleting the last of the junk emails when the stairs creaked under hesitant, labored steps. Irena dropped the laptop and leapt for the door, opening it just as her roommate appeared at the top of the steps. Nyissana's face was grey under her bright purple hair. The scar from her forehead and across the bridge of her nose stood out in stark relief. Her eyes were closed to slits, with moisture weeping from the right one. She leaned heavily against the wall, favoring her right leg.

Irena rushed to her side, helped her across the living area and into her room. She eased Nyissana carefully onto her pillows and was reaching for the migraine pills in the nightstand. Her hand grabbed the nasal inhaler when she saw Nyissana's hand twitch a certain way. She waited until Nyissana had let out her breath, then depressed the plunger as she inhaled sharply. The injector went into the trash as she closed the door and settled onto the bed next to her friend, taking her hand when she started to relax.

The touch of Nyissana's hand pulled her back from the edge of sleep. Her voice was shaky as she said, "Thanks. I forgot to refill my desk stash. Could you get me a muscle relaxer? My leg is killing me today."

"Water or ouzo?" Irena asked.

"Water, I have to go back, once I recover. Sophia has gone missing and we have some kind of big meeting tomorrow. Jim's being more secretive than usual, but he wants our demo package updated with things from the latest Conflict for Mars updates and everyone is stressed." Irena rose, grabbed a pill from the bathroom, and returned with water. "Thank you, my goddess!"

Irena smiled and softly kissed Nyissana's scar. "You can go back, but only after you can stand."

"Yes, goddess."

They lay together in the dark room for a long while before Nyissana eased herself up. "I think I'm ok enough now." She stood, swayed a tiny bit, then walked, mostly steadily, to the bathroom.

"What do you mean Sophia's 'gone missing'? Like out sick with an extended illness?"

"I wish. No, 'gone missing' as in not at her apartment, not with friends or family, not in the hospitals or morgue, not anywhere we can find her. She was working on ideas for a Conflict for Mars community event, but hadn't presented them yet."

Irena sat upright. "Have you called the cops? Has anyone been to her place?"

"Andrew lives near her and stopped by yesterday on his way in. Her car was in her spot and her apartment looked fine from the outside. When did she last stop in at Waverly's?"

"Four nights ago. She seemed ok. Well, as ok as she has been since coming back from Lefronica."

"I wish she'd tell us what happened out there. Did you notice anything when she left? Anyone following her or showing undue attention?"

"No, she just left, got in her car, and drove away." Irena was quiet a few moments, thinking of the friendly and out-going Sophia that had gone to Lefronica, to work in digital effects, and has returned quiet, withdrawn, and haunted.

"She didn't come to work Monday or email, which was strange. She's really good at letting everyone know if she won't be in. No one's called the police yet, none of us are related to her and her family doesn't seem to care."

"Yeah, they haven't played a part in her life for years. They disowned her during her senior year."

"What?"

"I don't know details, but it must have been weird, I can't imagine Sophia doing anything all that bad. She had just started to open up again, when we'd talk at the bar. I was hoping she'd finally say what happened in Lefronica before she came back."

"I know. I'd like to hurt the people that damaged her. She's one of the nicest people I know. Well, I've got to get back and see if we can get the presentation package together for tomorrow." Nyissa slowly headed for the door.

"Call me if you need anything or want me to help you home."

Irena waited until Nyissana was gone before she ran to her room, threw on black jeans and a loose black sweat shirt, grabbed her bag from the floor where she had dropped it, and dumped it on the pile of clothes on the bed. Rummaging through the clutter, she found the card Sophia had handed her four nights ago. On the back was a phone number and an address. She yanked open the bottom drawer of her desk and grabbed the small leather pouch with lock tools in it and dropped them into her bag. She scooped up the rest of the contents and dumped them in as well and headed for the door, stopped, dashed back to the kitchen, grabbed a couple pairs of latex gloves, then headed out. She took the tunnel from Waverly's basement to the parking ramp under CyCore Games, slid into Nyissana's little black sports car, fumbled the racing harness closed, and headed into the night.

Twenty minutes later Irena pulled into Sophia's apartment complex, startled by the cheap hotel appearance of it. It was a three story U shaped building, with parking under the units and a small pool in the center of the square. A large rental office sat near the entry to the lot. Each apartment opened onto an outdoor walkway with concrete stairs to the parking area. Some tenants had flower pots attached to their railings and sitting near their doors. Sophia's area was not one of them.

Before leaving the car she slipped on both sets of gloves, pulled the lock picks out of their pouch, and wrapped her left hand loosely around them. Slowly she climbed the concrete and steel steps to the third floor balcony and walked to the door marked 212. The training came back quickly and within seconds the lock clicked open.

Inside the cheap hotel look was magnified. A single room contained a simple bed, dresser, a grey metal desk with monitors on it, and a tiny kitchenette to the back. A microscopic bathroom occupied the back right corner. The only nice things in the place were a large ebony wardrobe against the bathroom wall, a very comfortable looking desk chair, and two new computer systems. Everything looked neat and orderly with no signs of struggle or disturbance. The black bed spread was pulled back and the sheets rumpled as if someone had gone to sleep but no clothes were scattered about. The towels in the bathroom were neatly placed on the racks and the sink and counter clean.

A large selection of gothic clothing hung in the wardrobe and a check book without a paper register sat in the desk drawer, amid numerous pens and sticky note pads. Bank statements were neatly filed in the left hand drawer, revealing a very large bank balance. Software design guides and reference manuals were arranged by subject in a cheap metal book case to the right of the desk.

Irena sat in the desk chair and started the computers. Windows full of code and notes popped into view once the systems had loaded. The character descriptions and scenario notes she understood, the code bits meant nothing. She reached for the power switch, then stopped when she remembered Nyissa's comments. Taking data disks from one of the desk drawers, she made two copies of all the directories that looked like they might be game related. It took most of the meager stock to get all of it from both machines.

Once the backups were done she turned the systems off and headed for the door. A glint from the bed caught her eye. Pulling the covers back farther she found a fist size, heart shaped sapphire sitting in the center of the bed. A strange shift of shadow prompted her to pick it up. A sound, part way between a heavy bass thump and a deep gong, so deep it was almost a vibration, echoed through the room. The lights flashed and flickered until the sound faded, leaving a strange stillness behind.

The gem rested quietly in her hand, giving off warmth and a faint heartbeat-like vibration. She stared at it for a long time before dropping it into a pocket. It seemed familiar, like an almost forgotten movie, hovering at the back of her thoughts. With a sigh, she stood, closed and locked the door, and returned to the car.

Back under the gaming studio she debated going up and giving the disks to Nyissa or waiting until she came home. In the end, she walked back to the apartment and dropped the disks on the kitchen counter. She pulled her phone out and dialed Nyissa's number. She answered on the fourth ring with, "You ok?"

"I have something that might help with your work. I can bring it over."

"What would that be?"

"Sophia's notes."

"How did you get those? Who let you in?"

"I let myself in. There's no sign of her. The bed looked slept in, but everything else was tidy."

"I forgot your talent with locks. Why are you still at Waverly's instead of locksmiting?"

"Waverly's pays much better with the tips and I like the music and the people."

"Bring it over and I'll meet you at the door."

"How about I bring it up instead and save your leg? I'll see you in five."

"Ok."

Irena picked up the disks and headed down the stairs from the apartment. She went all the way to the basement, back to the tunnel to the garage, then over to the door marked CyCore Game Employees Only, where she used her pass card to get through. Upstairs was another card door, then she headed up more stairs to the programing offices on the top floor. Nyissana wasn't the only person working late. A few looked up until they recognized her, then went back to work.

She found Nyissana, in her cluttered office, peering into her monitor, her skin still a bit grey in the bluish light. Nyissana slid the disks into her system, copying each one, then started to look through them. She smiled a few minutes later. "Thanks! This is exactly what we were missing."

"There was something else in her room. It seems familiar, but I don't know why." Irena pulled the gem out of her pocket and handed it to her friend. "I found this in the middle of Sophia's bed, sitting under the pulled back sheets. It made a deep gong, boom, reverberation, I don't know quite how to explain it, when I first touched it."

Nyissa stared at the gem for a long while, then said, "Jaime's game. Remember the Summoning Gem for the magical Hut? This looks like her description, if I remember right. But how and why was this in her bed? A sapphire that large would cost a fortune. What's going on?"

"I have no idea."

"What happened to Sophia?"

"Was there anybody that didn't like her?"

"Not here. As far as I know everyone was quite happy with her and with her work. Were there any odd or creepy types paying too much attention at Waverly's?"

"No. She'd come in, have a couple and leave. After we started talking she'd have three or four and stay longer, but I never saw anyone chatting her up."

"Jim is going to call the police tomorrow. Maybe they can find something. You might be prepared, I'm sure they'll want to talk to us all."

"I need to head home. I have the mid afternoon shift to cover and a bunch of monthly reports to finish up in the morning."

"See you sometime tomorrow, then."

"Call if you need anything."


Dreams 1

Irena stripped off her top and slid the jeans down. She was asleep as her head hit the pillow. Lavender edged dreams drifted through her slumber, barely memorable until a voice echoed in a large, empty space. "There is only one supreme truth of the multi-verse and that is the multi-verse exists. All the other "truths", concepts, and ideas are the wish dreams of the participants, trying to understand something that just is. Which means the "job" of the Sisters is simply to make sure the multi-verse continues to be."

"How does the balance play into that?" a voice asked from the darkness to the right. The dreams shifted before the answer was heard, back into chaotic half images and impressions.

Irena awoke groggy and fuzzy. The hot shower barely helped and the coffee just cleared her vision. She staggered down to Waverly's a little after ten and settled herself in the office. The weekly papers, reports, and forms waited in their neat piles.


Police

Irena was behind the bar when the police came into Waverly's. The lunch rush was just winding down when Leslie, the hostess at the door, directed the two officers to the bar. One was in uniform, the other was more casual. "Excuse me, Ms. Valinskya?"

"Yes, officers?"

"I'm Detective Klein and this is Officer Hanreson. Do you have a few moments?"

"What can I do for you?"

"We are looking into the disappearance of a Ms. Nelson and would like to ask you a few questions."

"Shall we talk in the office?" Irena asked. She led them through the half full dining area, through the kitchen, and into the back storage areas where her office was. It was small, with almost enough room for a large desk, two filing cabinets, and a couple of chairs. Invoices and other paperwork covered the desk. Wire trays, stacked two high, sat on each side of the desk. One was labeled Waverly's the other O'Malley's. The walls to the right and left of the desk each had a large cork board with similar labels, Waverly's to the right and O'Malley's to the left. Each one held staff schedules, notes between staff, and other items central to keeping the bars running. A large white board behind the desk held less permanent notes about coming events and band appearances.

"You manage both Waverly's and O'Malley's?" Detective Klein asked.

"Yes, I do. We expanded a couple of years ago."

Hanreson nodded, "I remember. My compliments on the authenticity of O'Malley's. It reminds me of a place just down from my grandparents in Dublin."

"Thank you. That's what Tom O'Malley was going for."

"May we get to the questions?" Detective Klein asked.

"Of course," Irena replied.

"Sure, boss," Hanreson added.

"You last saw Ms. Nelson five nights ago, here at Waverly's, is that correct?" Irena nodded. "Did she seem distraught, agitated, or nervous in any way?"

"Not at all. She had a couple of drinks, she talked about her day, then she left."

"What did she say about her day?"

"To me, it sounded the same as always, too many deadlines and too much stress, but exciting as well."

"Was anyone taking undo interest in her? Anyone watching her that would have left right after her maybe?"

"No. She sat at the bar, two stools over from the waitress station, if I remember right, and had three, no, two drinks, Kahlua and creams. She talked to me a bit about her day, then left."

"Do you remember when she left?" Hanreson asked.

"It was before midnight, but I'm not certain exactly. A crowd of loud frat boys came in just before midnight and one was sitting where she had been."

"Do you always remember what people have to drink?" Klein asked.

"If they are friends or regular patrons, yes. Sophia was a regular and a friend, always sat at the bar, but had just started to talk a bit more over the last few months. She seemed reasonably happy, but lonely. Even so, she didn't strike up conversations with the sharks that sometimes come here. We had been good friends before she left here for almost a year. When she came back, she was a lot more quiet and wthdrawn. I was hoping she would tell me what had happened."

"Do you think someone followed her back and was either stalking or threatening her?"

"Not that I saw at the bar. If it were happening elsewhere, well, she didn't say anything about it. Is there any way you could find out?"

"Depends,if it was happening and there was evidence in her possession, we could investigate. Would you know of anyone who had keys to her place? Did she ever mention a boyfriend or other close relationship?"

"No. She talked a bit about dating, trying to find a boyfriend, but it sounded like she wasn't having any luck. Might I ask why?"

"Just exploring all options. Right now it looks like she just ran away."

"Without telling any of her friends or co-workers?"

"We see this a lot. Young, professional person gets in over their head, either socially or financially, then runs rather than try to sort it out. If their behavior changed, then it's even more likely."

"Was she in financial trouble? She always paid here."

"I'm sorry, I can't discuss that."

"Oh, right. Of course."

"I think that's all for now. If you think of anything out of the ordinary, please give me a call," Detective Klein said as he handed her a card. "Oh, one more thing, do you know where she lives?"

"Out on Kingfisher St., Happy Holiday apartments. She had me call her a cab a couple of times. I'm sorry, I don't remember the apartment number. This was a new place, after her return."

"That's ok. Her work gave us that."

"Should I be worried? My roommate works where she does and said I might have been the last person to see her."

"No, I don't think so. Like I said, it looks like she just ran off. Shame to abandon her stuff, but many do that."

"Abandon her stuff?"

"Looks like most of her things are still in the apartment. Odd about the computers, though."

"How so, if I might ask?"

"She had two of them for starters and they were both wiped clean, the drives blanked so far they wouldn't even boot. Forensics has them and we'll see what can be recovered."

"Is that odd?"

"For a runner, yes. Most would have just left them. Wouldn't necessarily have made the bed or straightened up the bathroom either. Now I've said more than I should. It's been good talking with you, Ms. Valinskya."

"I have a vacation planned. Will it be alright for me to leave town?"

"That shouldn't be a problem. You could give me your mobile number in case I need to ask you anything else." Irena gave him the number and watched as he added it to the notes. Once he was finished, they left and Irena returned to work.


Jaime Arrives

Nyissa came into Waverly's while Irena was talking to one of the bartenders. She was trailed by someone Irena thought she should know. They waved and crossed to the bar. "Irena, look who I found roaming the halls of CyCore. You remember Jaime, right?"

"Damn, it's been too long! I didn't recognize you in that corporate garb you are wearing." Jaime was dressed in a power suit and had her hair pulled back in a severe bun. Up close, Irena could see the three studs above Jaime's right eye brow.

She made a face and replied, "Yeah, it's not normal for me, but I wanted to put Jim into a spin and it worked. I am here with the Rani Corp executive team to negotiate a new game project with CyCore. The mega-corp wants to fund creating a massively multi player game based on the Realms of the Hut."

"Wow that is extremely cool! How did it go?"

"It's a long story. Nyis was thinking I could crash in your spare apartment for a few days so we could catch up. Would that be ok?"

"Sure, that'd be awesome. You want food, drinks? I can easily comp you."

"Food and drinks, but let me put it on the corp card. They won't care, especially when I tell them it's for friends. How much longer do you have?"

"About another hour. Just need to make sure everything is covered for the evening."

They headed up to the apartment as soon as Irena was done. The spare apartment was at the back of the building, just down the hall from Nyis and Irena's. Jaime threw her gear in the spare apartment and swiftly changed into a loose top and sweats. She let her wavy deep brown hair was down, and it framed her elfin face. "You were going to say how it went," Irena reminded her as they headed back to Irena and Nyissana's apartment.

"Truthfully, it was complicated. Sorry Nyis, but your boss, Jim, is a hyper-conservative prude."

"I know, but he treats his staff well."

"A prude? He came across as a bit of a horn-dog when I went to the company party last year," Irena added.

"He's that way in his personal life, but he won't do nudity, sexual innuendo, or much risque content in the games we do," Nyissana explained. "Jaime and the corp pitched Realms of the Hut as an adult, free to play game, fully funded by her company and Jim went ballistic. He swore up and down that he wouldn't let his company put its name on an adult title and that free to play was the kiss of death for a game, never mind evidence to the contrary."

"We were hoping to negotiate a simple contract development project today and start talking details tomorrow, but it looks like it won't be that simple. Vashi and the corporate executive team will present him with three options in the morning. He can accept our proposal for contract development and make lots of money, he can refuse and we will poach his staff and bury him in legal action until he licenses the technology to us, or we can buy his company out from under him. Vashi typically goes with the buy-out option. It is more complicated since CyCore went public a few years ago and Rani Corp has always remained a private venture. We have very deep pockets, however, and many share holders just want good returns."

"Vashi?" Irena asked.

"Vashi Ristan, owner and CEO of Rani Corp."

"The Vashi Ristan that is Matriarch of Tzanistan? Holy shit." Irena remembered what she had found when looking up Maharaja Resorts, a division of Rani Corp, where her younger sister started working. The little information available on the privately held Rani Corp hinted that their budget was larger than many countries. It's corporate offices were located in Tzanistan, a section of Nehtan given to Vashi as her own country. Meriksan government departments had gone head to head with Rani Corp several times because Rani Corp would not allow the Meriksan government to tap communications through their network systems. Rani Corp continued to insist on warrants and then only for specific individuals, never a blanket watch. Prior to the fight, many groups had competing network access, but the ones that automatically allowed government monitoring lost all their market when Rani Corp remained firm. What had impressed her the most was the rates hadn't gone up when World Telecom and Technology, a wholly owned division of Rani Corp, became a basic monopoly.

She had drifted out of the conversation, so hadn't heard anything until Jaime was saying, "...and since you both know so much about the Realms of the Hut, I would like you to form the core of the local development group."

"Uh, what? Sorry, drifted out for a minute there."

"You and Nyis should be the directors for the game development. You both have extensive knowledge from before. Once we have Non-Disclosure Agreements from you, I will give you the entire data package."

Irena froze for a moment, then replied, "I would be happy to consult, play-test, and provide creative input, but I don't know if I want to change jobs. Let me think about it over a few days. I have a trip planned for the next two weeks, which would give me time to consider. Would that be ok?"

"Of course. Either way, if you sign the NDA, I can share the details to help you decide. Now, I think I need to sleep. I'm still getting used to a twelve hour time change."

"You have one with you I can sign?" Irena asked. Jaime pulled two out of her bag.

"If you have portable memory systems, I'll transfer the data." Nyissana pulled two out of a stack of stuff shoved into the corner of a table by the couch.

Irena's phone rang while Jaime was transferring files. "Tom?"

"Irena, are you on the road or still in town?"

"I was going to leave in the morning."

"Good! Could I come over for a few minutes? I have something I'd like to ask you before you leave."

"Sure. Just so you know, Nyis is here with our friend Jaime."

"That's ok. See you in a bit," Tom said as there was a knock at the door. Nyissa opened it to see a smiling Tom standing outside. "Said I'd see you in a bit."

"Come in," Nyissa said. "Jaime, this is Tom. Tom owns the bar and this building, as well as the CyCore Games building and a bunch of other stuff. Tom, Jaime is an old friend from university and is here visiting."

"Pleased to meet you," Tom said as he came in. He was a powerful looking man, with greying hair, the start of a paunch, and lively eyes. He was carrying a small earthenware jug, a bundle of papers, and was wearing his trademark woolen coat. He lifted the jug and motioned at the table, "Glasses? This is the private reserve from my family's distillery in Eirleandra." He smiled at their looks and continued, "I've come to offer Irena a business proposal. Irena, you don't have to answer it tonight, but please think on it. I am getting older, no, don't shake your head, we all know it's true. I have no one to pass the business on to, so I propose to sell it to you. The sale and price would be arranged to minimize taxes and fees. Verne, the company accountant, has some very good strategies in mind. You would become the sole owner of the bars, the properties, my house, and everything else. I'd ask that we split the profits in half and that I am allowed to remain in my house until my demise."

Irena almost dropped the glasses she was getting at his statements. "Are you serious, Tom? The company has been your entire life."

"Of course I'm serious. I'd give it to you, but the taxes on that kind of gift would kill the deal. You took my small, struggling, neighborhood tavern and turned it into a thriving business. I would have closed it down five years ago if you and Nyissa hadn't taken it on and turned it into what we have today. You have demonstrated an excellent understanding of business and I think you can make Grainne Ni Mhaille Holdings even stronger." He poured generous amounts of amber liquid into the glasses provided and lifted his, "To new opportunities!"

Irena hesitantly lifted her glass, tapped it with Tom's. Nyissana stared for a moment, then tapped hers against the other two. Jaime hastily moved her glass in as well. They each took a careful taste. "It's an awesome offer, Tom. I would be honored to take it."

"You know, Tzanistan has very favorable tax laws. If you transferred registry of the business to Tzanistan, you could sell to Irena for next to nothing, without penalty. The transfer is a bit convoluted for an existing company, but it is simpler if it is a privately held company. Is that the case?"

"yes, it is," Tom replied.

"I could put our Chief Financial Officer and Legal Head in touch with your legal people and see if it would help you," Jaime commented.

"I would be very interested in that. I'll give you Hanna's number. She's head of legal. If Irena were blood family, it would be a bit easier, but still very costly. As for running the company, there's not much more to the task than what you do anyway, just a bit more tax and property items to keep track of. The property managers are all very competent, so it's mostly oversight and final decisions. Right now, Verne and the accounts guys keep tabs on the day to day expenses and prepare the tax reporting and pay-outs. We can do the final sign-off when you get back and after I talk to the people Jaime mentioned."

Tom stayed a little longer, talking with Jaime and Nyissa while Irena drifted in and out of the conversation, still in shock from the offer. Tom got up to leave when Jaime headed off to sleep.
