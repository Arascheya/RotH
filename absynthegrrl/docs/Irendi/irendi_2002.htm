Finally, in desperation, she mixed up one of her grandmother's herbal concoctions to try and calm me down. I don't know what was in it, but within minutes the shakes had stopped and the nausea was fading. About twenty minutes later I was eating and feeling fine.

I didn't think much more about it until I reached for the aluminum cook pot when I was putting away the dishes the next day. When my hand touched the aluminum of the pot I yanked it back as pain flashed again. "DAMN, I'll learn, really I will. I touched the pot again," I replied to her worried look. Something clicked then as I remembered the soda we'd split early yesterday, the one in the aluminum can. "Valarie..."

Valarie suddenly smiled and reached into the refrigerator. She grabbed the jar of herb tea from the previous night and shoved my fingers into it. Immediately the pain stopped and the grey, burned skin started to regenerate. After a few minutes my fingers were normal again. "It's going to be really hard for you not to come in contact with aluminum. Especially in places where you can't see it."

We spent the rest of the day experimenting. If I managed to drink some of the herb tea after contact with aluminum, then I was fine. Even a small bit would counteract the effects. It worked especially well if I had ingested aluminum contaminated liquids. I found that I could feel a burning sensation if I concentrated a bit on the liquid I was swallowing. For contact damage, topical application was better if possible. We found that anything covering my skin was sufficient protection from contact. We also found that even a little of the herb mixture in a hand cream base would work as well as liquid, making it much easier to carry around.


Monday I found flowers on my desk along with a brief note from Jeff inviting me to lunch. It was written with a subtext that implied I should not refuse. I wanted to talk to Traci about it, but didn't, remembering her injunctions about bugs anywhere near my spaces. I assumed that she would also be wired, given we were known to spend time together.

Lunch was a constant trial. His small talk was all about his achievements, plans, and money. If I had been listening from another booth I'd have thought he was giving his biography. Several times he tried to draw me out about my relationships with Valarie and Traci, once more 'warning' me against Traci. As we were finishing a desert that I could almost feel clogging my arteries, one of the wait staff came over with a bouquet of flowers. Jeff smiled his most charming and predatory smile as he presented them to me. "Here, something to remind you of me while I'm out of town." He insisted on taking my arm as we walked back. I rolled my eyes as we passed Traci's cubicle. He didn't release me until we arrived at my work area, after passing through most of the rest of the place. A grin twitched my mouth as I realized he'd just tried to 'mark' me as his. He must have seen the flicker of it as he released me because he smiled and said, "Try not to miss me too much while I'm gone."

"I don't think it'll be a problem." He looked almost puzzled for a brief instant, then smiled and headed to his office. Something made me glance back toward the copier where I saw Jack standing almost out of sight in the doorway. The smile he sent me couldn't have been called friendly.


The next Monday found me sitting in another one of Jeff's favorite romantic restaurants listening to him babble about his life. After a time he said, "I've talked about me both times now. How about we talk about you. I've heard some very nasty rumors about you and that woman you are sharing a home with. Now I could see where you'd be curious about other lifestyles after having Alan suddenly snap like he did, but I think you should know, all it really takes is the right man. I'm sure that you'd be able to settle down and raise a nice family if you just would let yourself go and take what's offered. Who knows, maybe something could be done about that ugly bit of business going on at the office. I know you couldn't possibly be involved in anything as dirty as espionage. I'm certain I could convince the investigators that you couldn't have been the one."

"There are laws against this type of harassment..."

"There are also laws against international espionage. I believe they are a bit more severe than the harassment you could claim. Besides, whom would they believe, a suspected spy or the director of a prestigious military contractor? Come, come, De, save yourself the hassle, admit that you find me attractive and it'll all work out."

"I might have admitted it once, when Alan, you and I first met, but now that I've seen how you behave, I can't. Good day to you." I shoved my chair back and started to stand.

"Jack was right about you, you're just another male hating bitch. You'll be sorry for this. I'll make you pay, just like the others." He was still ranting as I walked away, trying to ignore the stares focused on him and watching me also.

By the time I reached the office I was so angry I was shaking. I wanted to tear him apart and feel his blood flow. I wanted to shred his already fragile ego. I stalked in, told my team I'd see them tomorrow, and prepared to head home. Traci caught me as I was just starting my bike. "De, I'll come over after work. There are a few more people I need to see, but I think you'll find what I've found to be very interesting."

It was a quick trip home. I placed a call to Valarie and told her not to meet me, that I was already home, then had her patch me into one of the center's phone counselors. I talked and ranted for about an hour. The woman on the other end had several good ideas about where to call for ways to deal with the harassment I was getting.

Valarie arrived home but Traci still hadn't shown up by 9 pm. Worried, I called her apartment and got her machine. We were just beginning to consider our next steps when Traci pulled up. "Sorry I'm late but I needed to talk to Alan and had to wait till after dinner hours to see him. You know, it's such a nice night, why don't we all go for a bike ride? I haven't been on a bike in years and I really miss it."

Valarie realized what was going on seconds before I did and started digging for a spare helmet for Traci. As she reached for her leathers, I caught the idea and enthusiastically agreed. Within minutes we were screaming off into the night. We slid to a stop in the middle of our field and dropped down to lay in the tall grass.

"What I found brings up more questions than it answers. Over the past several years there has been a sporadic trickle of information out of the company. Every so often internal security is alerted and tries to find it. Usually they begin by questioning the directors and division heads. Then they get to Jeff and Jack and invariably then target in on one or two employees. Most of the time the employees are women who have turned Jeff down and who are known or suspected of being sexual minorities of some type. The worst case was two years before I started when the target was a post-op transsexual woman working in weapons development. By the time the smoke cleared she had committed suicide, her husband was in a mental hospital, and their adopted children had been turned over to the state. Two internal security people had died in mysterious accidents and the company head office dropped the investigation. In each case the investigation has been dropped as soon as all leads have been checked. In all cases the missing data was only accessible to a small number of people in this division. There must actually be a leak, just not where everyone ends up looking."

"Babe, who has access to your files?"

"I do, and my team does, why?"

"What about Jeff or Jack?"

"They could certainly get to the machines, but it would be hard for them to gain access, not impossible, just hard. It would fit, but how have they so easily escaped notice?" I stopped suddenly as I caught a faint sound off to our right. In a flash I changed, forgetting Traci in my haste. In moments I was off, tracking the scent on the breeze. A quick pounce dropped the lurker and my roar brought the others running. Valarie questioned him while I periodically made threatening moves and sounds. He babbled rather quickly that he had been hired by a nameless man to track us and record anything we said. His small tape recorder was quickly disposed of. A little more persuasion pried from him the contact point where he would receive his drops. Then we had a big problem, what to do with our watcher. After a lot of debate we decided to have him stay at our place as a guest, at least until we tracked back the next person in the chain. Carefully Traci and Valarie loaded him aboard Valarie's bike and they headed back. I sprinted back taking a short cut and was there waiting for them.

We left him in the basement, tied securely to the water pipes. Paranoid that Valarie was she had nothing on Traci who tied him in a way where he could reach nothing that could help him escape. She apologized profusely to him all the while. Then she turned to me, "What the hell are you?"

Valarie and I tried to explain what little we knew. She listened quietly, asking questions every once in a while. "So Alan wasn't crazy after all."

"No, not entirely...I wish there was some way I could get him out without having to become a lab rat somewhere..." Every time I thought about him it hurt. He wasn't really a bad sort and he didn't deserve to be held for something that wasn't his fault, yet I couldn't figure a way of telling the authorities without trading myself for him. I liked him and all, but I wasn't about to trade places.

The next morning Traci and I headed into work while Valarie called in sick so she could watch the drop. We timed our arrivals to match our usual schedules so Traci was already there when I walked in. Jeff met me at the door flanked by two security guards. "Irendi Walker if you would be so kind as to hand over your keys and security card, you have been relieved of your position. You may now leave. You will receive your back salary and a small severance package. Under the current circumstances I would recommend that you do not leave the area as a full national security council investigation is under way. You are under suspicion of international espionage. Good day Ms. Walker."
