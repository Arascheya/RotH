Anastasia - Chapter 1

Anastasia slowly woke to the rocking and clacking of the train's motion. "Mother? Where are we going?" She murmured softly, eyes still closed. As her drowsiness faded she opened her eyes on unexpected face. The woman was dressed as a proper British Lady, similar to some of the ones that used to come to court. She was seated near the bed, watching Anastasia as she stirred.

"Ah, you awaken. You had me worried for a moment."

Anastasia's eyes darted about, taking in the rich hangings and dimly lit air of the small compartment. The woman was speaking to her in flawless Russian with just the faintest tinge of a British accent. Uncertainty rose rapidly as Anastasia struggled to remember why she might be on a train and sleeping. Dim, dark memories surged just below her awareness. "Who are you and what is going on?" A tinge of near hysteria leaked threw around the words.

"My name is Charlotte and you are in a safe place. What do you remember?" The woman replied.

"What should I remember? What do you mean safe place? What Is Going On?!?"

"You don't remember anything then? You were in an...accident. I was nearby and stopped to help you. You are aboard my private rail car."

"I don't remember anything. Where is my family? Have you contacted them?"

"I'm afraid not. I can't really do that without knowing who you are now can I?"

"I am...I..." Her face clouded up as she struggled to remember her name. "What has happened? Please, tell me!" Her eyes grew calculating as she saw a small look of worry cross Charlotte's face. "There's something you aren't telling me, isn't there?"

"Yes, I'm afraid there is." Charlotte drew a deep breathe, paused, then said slowly, "Your name I believe to be Anastasia. You were killed in an unfortunate series of events." She held up her hand as Anastasia drew breath to speak. "Wait please. Let me try and finish. What I said is true. You are safe here. You do need to make a choice though. One that you probably aren't ready to make."

"What...what choice?" Anastasia choked out through a suddenly paralyzed throat. Icy panic crawled up her back as she remembered all the skipped confessions and the thoughts about boys. Dark memories crept closer as well. Screams, shots, flaring candles. Terror curled through the dark in foggy tendrils. "Not hell, please, not hell. I can hear the screams..."

"That isn't for me to decide. The offer I am allowed to make is only this. You may come with me and step away from the beliefs of your world or accept the beliefs of your up-bringing and whatever they say will befall you. If you accept my offer you will join the ranks of a place called Eternia. You will help in the saving of the beauty of the Realms until such time as you fall or move on."

"Fall? Move on? Eternia? Get behind me, Satan." Anastasia struggled to sit up.

For a brief moment Charlotte looked surprised, then she burst out laughing. "I'm sorry, that wasn't very proper. You just startled me. I am no devil or demon. I am no angel or saint either. I am only a humble messenger with an offer from the Lady of Eternia. She is beyond the pettiness of the godlings that preach heaven, hell, Satan, and all the rest of that nonsense." Gently she pushed Anastasia back onto the bed. "You've had a bad shock. You'll be up and moving soon, but it's better not to push too hard."

"Let me go!"

"If that is what you truly want, I will. Just rest a few minutes more and I'll take you back."

"Back?"

"It wasn't safe to remain where you were killed. I had to move, but I can take you back. Just be sure that is what you want because once you step off this train I can make no other offers. If you are worried about hell you might want to think about this quite carefully." She smiled slightly, "Personally, I find great comfort in being able to side-step the religion of my past. I did a lot of things that were quite questionable and not having to worry does bring a delightful sense of peace."

"You did? What, if I might ask?"

"You may ask," Charlotte replied.

Anastasia laughed as she said, "But you are not going to tell me."

"Possibly, but first you need to make your decision."

"How about you tell me and then I decide? It might help me with my choice."

Charlotte responded with an exaggerated frown, "Well, as long as you know, this just is not proper." Her teasing smile took any sting out of the words. "I had sex out of marriage, for fun, profit, and to acquire positions. I have stolen food when I was hungry. I have lusted in my heart after women and men, and acted on a few of those cases. I have not killed anybody, however. Oh, and I am a witch. At least I would be called one for the visions I sometimes get. They did help for the small time I made a living giving card readings to the fancy ladies of London."

Anastasia eyed her speculatively. "But you look the proper lady now. You were not noble born then?"

Charlotte laughed again. "No. I hope that does not cause you difficulty. Where we are going birth rank is not important. Only what you do determines how you are regarded. That point does cause trouble for some. Are you one of them?"

"I," Anastasia paused, "I am not certain. I have no memory that would help me know. Are you able to tell me anything of my past?"

"I am not allowed by the rules of Eternia. If your past is important to you, you will need to remember it for yourself."

"Is this normal, to forget like this?"

"Not always. It does seem to be most common when severe trauma has occurred. I do not wish to anger you, however, you must give me a decision. Soon the godlings will send someone to collect you. If you have not decided by then I must let them take you. Then you will be bound by the beliefs of your past, even if you no longer remember all of them."

Anastasia hesitated before slowly saying, "I accept your offer."

"Do you do so willingly?"

"I accept willingly and without pressure or persuasion."

"Welcome to the Death City Metro and to Eternia, Anastasia." Charlotte pulled open a drawer on the small table next to the bed. She picked up an oddly shaped bit of dark material and spoke at it. "We may be on our way. Anastasia has accepted our offer."

A voice replied from within it, startling Anastasia. "Excellent. Arrival at the Portal will be in three hours." The sounds of the rails changed slightly, as if the train had passed onto a different stretch of track.

"Where did that voice come from? What's in that box?"

"This is a form of the magic from Eternia. It is called a 'com' and I am told it comes from a dead world. It was regarded as an every day item there apparently. It allows me to speak with the driver of the train."

Anastasia slowly lifted herself to a sitting position in the bed and reached for the drawn curtains. "Where are we now?"

"You might not want to do that, unless you want another shock." Charlotte said quietly. Anastasia shot her an angry look and whipped the curtains aside. A small shriek escaped her as she stared into the darkness beyond. Stars sprinkled the Void around the hurtling train. White and shaking she dropped back down on the bed. "I did try and tell you. We are in the lands of magic now. Nothing is quite as it was in the Realms. In three hours you will see one of the greater forms of magic. It is the Portal into Eternia from the area of the Realms." She held up her hand as Anastasia started to speak. "Please, do not ask me what that means. I can say the words but I have no understanding of this either. In the time I have been in Eternia I have learned the only way to not become deranged is to accept even the things which seem impossible. Sometimes understanding will come after time."

"What...what is that outside?" The hysteria was back with more force this time.

"That is what is between the Realms. It is called 'Void' or 'space' or 'aether'. We travel across it to get to the various places where we will be working. We collect things when they would otherwise be destroyed. Then we will slip them back into the various Realms where they can continue to be appreciated. We are often the reason you can find that perfect item in the back corner of a small shop somewhere. Something that no one remembered was there." Anastasia was still white and shivering slightly. "I know you probably will not be able to, but you should try and sleep some more." Charlotte bit her lip and added slowly, "Your death was not easy or clean. Your spirit is going to need some time to recover, even if you do not consciously remember. I know some soothing songs if that would help you to sleep."

"Yes mother, please sing to me," Anastasia replied with just a hint of sarcasm. Charlotte began singing softly. Within moments Anastasia's eyes drifted closed and her breathing evened out into sleep.

The nightmares started almost immediately. Shadowy figures moved just beyond the lamp light in a dark, dank cellar. Heavy boots thumped on the upper floor and muffled curses drifted down the stairs. Wispy figures stood next to her. She tried to turn and see who was next to her but a tall shadow figure held her fast. She struggled, trying to get loose, trying to run to a tall shape nearby.

Anastasia jerked upright as a raw scream split the air. "Ah, no, no, Mother!" Sightless eyes darted around the room, not really seeing anything.

"Anastasia, wake up, wake up. You are safe."

At last the eyes settled onto Charlotte's face and some of the tension drained from the young woman. "Sleep does not seem to be something I can do right now. If you cannot speak about my past, maybe you could tell me a bit about your own?"

"I will, but there is not much to tell. I was born in London to a family of performers. I was the youngest of three sisters. My parents had a small act in one of the middle class theatres at the time where we would help with various small things. I first performed when I was eleven in a rope jumping skit with my sisters. As I grew older I managed to get and keep work through various methods for most of my early adulthood. I scratched and scraped for many years until I slept my way into a break. After that it got easier. There is very little of interest in my life."

"I doubt that. Being in London in the late 1800's would have to have been more interesting than you are making it. What did you do? Did you see Jack the Ripper?"

"See Jack the Ripper? No, and I never want to! That was a horrible time. Many of us were afraid to walk the streets at night."

"But it had to be exciting besides, did it not? I think I was kept at home or not allowed to see much. I think I was surrounded with expectations and rules that I did not understand."

"Exciting? It might have been, if it were possible to ignore the ever present fear. I did not really get over it until I traveled to America for a while in 1891."

"America? Land of decadence and ill repute? What was it like?" Anastasia's eyes shone with curiosity and hunger.

"It was interesting. I learned a couple of things to bring back and enhance my reputation. Otherwise I was in the large cities, so it was like London in many ways. From what I saw, London was easily as decadent in many ways as the places in America." Charlotte thought for a few minutes. "The most excitement I remember was in 1895. Through a strange series of events I had come to know Lord Willem Wellingham, the magnate behind International Transport Consortium, a huge group of related companies that have tendrils into most of Western Europe."

"I have heard of this company. It runs the Trans Siberian railroad under license from the Tsar of Imperial Russia. A ruthless but honorable man I am told."

"You remember something! Is there anything else?"

For a moment the dark shapes flitted across the edges of Anastasia's memory and then withdrew. "No, not really," she replied with a soft sigh. "So you know Lord Willem. What is he really like? Did you meet his daughter? I admire her. I wish I could have had her opportunities."

"I am not sure you would. In the summer of 1895 there were several very strange events. The biggest one was the disappearance of the entire Wellingham household for several days. The servants covered as well as they could, however, by the third day gossip throughout the city reported that the family could not be located. Those of us that were friends were not able to contact them either."

"Did you ever find out what happened?"

"Not completely, but I was able to find out a little bit about it. It all started with a new group of acquaintances that entered the scene. I am trying to remember exactly what happened. It was twenty-two years ago and I have experienced quite a bit since then."

"Lucy Wellingham was still single and having many parties to search for eligible suitors. Mina Harker, her friend has just had her husband disappear in the wilds of Transylvania, and there were rumors of another killer in Whitechapel. Lord Wellingham had made his first contribution to the Alhambra Theatre and his family had started to attend. I had met Lucy, Mina, and Lady Wellingham at one of the benefit events. I think Lucy liked me because I was not proper."

"A new killer in Whitechapel, other than the ripper?" Anastasia asked.

"Yes. This on left corpses drained of blood. Usually petty criminals and other undesirables. In some ways the police weren't all that interested in finding the perpetrater."

"After the benefit Lucy would come and see me at the theatre during the day. Sometimes she brought her friend Mina along. By that summer we were good friends. Sometimes they would invite me along to the fancy parties they attended. At first I thought it was just to shock the proper people until I realized Lucy and Mina would spend more time with me than with their peers. It was still not an acceptable situation to a great many of the others at the social level of the Wellinghams, but Lord Willem never tried to prevent it. I think he secretly enjoyed annoying the others with his eccentricities."

"Near the end of April in 1895 Lucy was having another party. This was the first time I was invited to the Wellingham estates. Mina and Lucy had coached me thoroughly on how to behave in their version of society. I always knew Lucy had a strange sense of humor, but I had never seen it at work as directly as I did that day. They sent a coach to pick me up at my flat near Leicester Square. Dressed in my finest I boarded it and was transported to the St. Pancras railway station. Confused, i was about to ask the driver what was going on, when a footman ushered me out of the cab. He conducted me through a private entrance to the station, through a maze of corridors, and out on a small private platform. Drawn up to the platform was a gleaming rail coach with the Wellingham arms blazoned on it. A small steam locomotive panted softly in the late afternoon sunlight.

The footman turned me over to a porter who helped me board the coach and settled me in an enormous arm chair. Silently he pulled a small lace covered table close to my left elbow and placed a crystal flagon of water and a single glass. Next came a vase with a single yellow rose. When he finally spoke it was to ask after my desires for drink and early dinner."

"The train left the platform as soon as the porter headed off to fill my desires. Within a few minutes he returned with the food and a fine red wine. Outside the train sped through London to the west and then turned onto a private siding that entered the Wellingham estate. It was an enormous amount of land carved out of the west end of London. There was a small private forest, riding pastures, private stables and signs of wealth and power I had never considered. The train pulled up to a platform at the side of the house where I was greeted by a smiling Lucy and Mina. 'We thought you should experience that at least once in your life,' Mina said as she helped me onto the platform."

"'Only very special friends of the family get to experience the private train. Welcome!' Lucy added as she pulled me into a close hug. 'Ready for the party?' I could only nod in shock."

Inside the grand ball room Lord and Lady Wellingham greeted me warmly as well. A large number of the wealthy and powerful of London society were there. 'Come with me, join the recieving line. There are more guests waiting to enter,' Lucy prompted when I started toward the rest of the guests."

"'Are you sure? I am not family.' I stammered.

"'Of course you are family,' Lucy corrected. Once we were ready Lord Wellingham nodded to the men at the door. They pulled them open and admitted the waiting guests. The names rolled over me in a constant stream until Lucy nudged me. 'Here come my suitors. I want you to tell me what you think of them at the end of the evening. That is one of the reasons I really wanted you here tonight.'"

"The voice of the announcer faded back into my awareness when I started to listen again. 'Dr. Jack Seward, chief resident, West End Asylum for the Insane. Mr....' The announcement stopped as Dr. Seward softly said something to him. Dr. Seward was a sallow, slightly stoop shouldered younger man. He peered out at the world through round rim glasses and hesitated slightly in his movements."

"'Dr. Seward has brought a guest. Introducing Dr. Henry Jekyll, resident at London Hospital and teaching surgeon from the Royal College of Surgeons.' A slightly older gentleman entered behind Dr. Seward. He smiled warmly and moved with ease into the crowd."

"'Mr. Quincy Adams, from America.'"

"'Isn't he adorable?' Lucy asked. Adams was tall, sun bronzed, and very handsome. He swept his large brimmed tan hat off and made a sweeping bow to the room."

"'Arthur Holmwood of the Lord Robert Holmwood family.'" Arthur was a typical London socialite. He was extremely well dressed and moved with a proper level of decorum and precision. His smile was friendly, if a bit distracted, and his eyes wnadered aimlessly around sizing up the other guests. Once he finished he moved toward the group he thought would bring the best prestige."

It took another half hour for the rest of the guests to be announced. Lucy moved off to mingle while Mina and I went for champaign. I kept a watch on the three suitors, noticing with whom they talked and how often Lucy flirted with each of them. As the evening wore on I noticed Lucy spending more and more time with Dr. Jekyll."

"The evening was a success for me as well. I had danced with several of the guests, been chatted up by some of the others, and had several interesting conversations with Lord and Lady Wellingham. As the time neared midnight the guests began to take their leave. I was on my way to ask Lord Wellingham about arrangements to return me to my flat when Lucy cornered me. 'Charlotte, you really must stay the night. We have so many empty rooms in this place. We can stay up late, talk about men, and eat chocolate. Please say you will stay!'"

"'I would love to stay.'"

"Half an hour later Mina, Lucy, and I were sitting on Lucy's bed and eating expensive Belgian chocolates. We talked about the party, the guests, some of the stranger fashions, and were just starting in on the topic of men. 'Charlotte, what did you think of my three suitors?'"

"'They were quite handsome and they seemed quite attentive.'"

"'I know that part. I meant what do you think of them as possible husbands.'" Lucy pressed.

"I tried to keep my answers evasive because I did not like the way any of them treated her. To a man they expressed the desire to protect and coddle her. The Lucy I knew would not last long in such circumstances. Lucy would not accept the evasive answers I gave her and kept asking until I told her my real thoughts."

"'Thank you, Charlotte. I do not know why you felt you could not say that to me from the start. I agree with you,' she sighed. 'I really was hoping Arthur would prove to be better than that. He has known me from childhood and I have never been one to settle back and be a pampered lady. Oh well, some can not escape their breeding. I had been going to decide on Arthur but your opinions as well as some other events have changed my mind. Mina, what did you think?'"

"'I have to agree with Charlotte. They all want to possesse you. I think none of them would want you to continue working for your father.'"

"'Other events?' I asked."

"'Yes, the introduction of the delightful Dr. Jekyll. Oh I know he seemed frightfully old by comparison, yet he speaks to me as an equal. Why we had several conversations just this evening about science and medicine. I have tried to speak with dear Jack about this in the past, but he shies away, desiring to preserve my delicate feelings.'"

"We spent the rest of the night gossiping, giggling, and having a wonderful time. In the morning there was a supremely decadent breakfast. Then Lucy and Mina accompanied me on the train back to London. They headed off to ITC while a cab returned me to my flat. It seemed small and shaby compared to the places I had been the following night."

"The next time I saw the entire family was at the grand opening of the Imhotep Exhibit at the British Museum. Some of us had been hired to provide a bit of entertainment at the banquet. It was all high class items and a bit different from our usual routines, however we were well paid. The Wellinghams were sitting with several people I had not seen before. Have you ever had the situation where you see someone and they make an immediate impression? That was the people with the Wellinghams. There were three of them. A dark haired, dark skinned woman who watched everything. I had seen that look in battle hardened soldiers but never in the face of a woman. At the time I thought she might have been Egyptian or maybe gypsy, later I was not as sure. The second one was dressed simply but carried herself with a sense of presence that would have made the Queen herself stop and bow. She had an aura of such intense power and control that you could see the servants change posture and attitude as they approached her. The last woman was the most memorable. She was rail thin and dressed like the others, yet there was some undefinable wrongness about her. Her eyes were often distant and empty. When she spoke it was in broken sentences and in a clipped jerky manner, like she never really learned to speak. She radiated menace in some way. The servants would barely approach her and everyone except her companions stayed as far away from her as they could."

"After the banquet the assemblage rose to view the new exhibit. The three women moved like predatory cats. Have you ever been to a large animal park and watched lions or tigers pace? These women moved in that way. Sinister and predatory with almost impossible grace. There was a step on the way to the exhibit hall that was incorrectly set. Almost everyone stumbled slightly on it. Those three glided over it as if it was not even there. I know I am rambling a bit, I just want to impart the entire strangeness of these people."

"When Lucy passed near me she waved me over and introduced them. Isabella Valen was the regal one. Letitia Thanatos was the soldier appearing one and the one with the name meaning death I have since learned. Shandi Kayara was the wrong one. They were all polite but distant and their eyes watched everything."

"I was about to reply when a faint whisper of a vision slipped by. I was not even sure it had happened until Shandi tilted her head, gave me a curious little half smile, and said, 'See later,' before gliding away. The other two looked slightly puzzled as they continued on."

"I turned to join my troupe in preparing to leave when Lucy put her hand on my arm and said, 'No, please come with us. I think you will find this interesting. There is some mystery behind this whole exhibit. This find is based on a family legend from a woman named Angelique Marsters. She is a friend of mine. I should bring her to lunch, I think the two of you would like each other. She is not proper either. Her family history says her mother is descended from a handmaiden in the service of Queen Hatshepsut and through this she had information on where the tomb of Imhotep was located. Of course no one believed her at first. About four years ago we started playing a game where she would predict what the latest Egyptologist would find where he was digging. Her success rate was amazing. After the fourth time in a row I asked her to talk to father. She told him what she knew about Imhotep and where he had been buried. Father financed part of the expedition and it was quite successful.'"

"Angelique Marsters was another curiosity of the time. In late 1888 her family was murdered while she was at the theatre with a suitor. When she returned home she found her father, mother and brother all dead in various sections of the house. All the bodies had been drained of blood through two small wounds on their necks."

"Angelique turned into a recluse. Before the deaths she was a well known socialite. She could be found at the best theatres and social places of the London nobility. After the death of her family she disappeared from view. She closed down the family home and rented a flat on Baker Street. She was rarely seen except at some of the charitable functions she continued in remembrance of her family. Lucy Wellingham and Mina Harker worked constantly to draw her out of hiding. They had little success until the appearance of the three mystery women."

"I asked Lucy why Angelique was not at the exhibit. She told me Angelique was not fond of public notice. Too many people still wanted to talk to her about the deaths of her family."

"Thinking about this so long after the fact I see things now I should have seen then. Like the strange looks Isabella and Letitia shared when Lucy mentioned the strange deaths of Angelique's family."

"The exhibit was interesting. It was more complete than many of the ones of the time. It looked like the tomb had been found in tact. I sometimes wish I could return to that Realm and see if I could read the hieroglyphics now. I have learned many languages in Eternia since I arrived. There was one small item, buried in the back, that was extremely odd. I would not have noticed it if Isabella had not seen it first. It looked like a scrap of papyrus that might have belonged to a student scribe. It contained several different sections of what looked like the shorthand hieroglyphics scribes would use in day to day work, at least that is what the information plaque said. What caught our eyes was the very small section that was in an entirely different script."

"I was just close enough to hear Isabella whisper to Letitia, 'I have seen that script before. And recently too.'"

"'Where?'"

"'While visiting She Who Must Never be Named.' Isabella replied. Letitia looked thoughtful at that news and I felt a chill at the way Isabella had spoken the name."

"I had reacted a bit more visibly than I had hoped because I saw Letitia start moving her hands and fingers in an elaborate dance. Isabella did as well. They continued their conversation in some type of finger-speech as they continued through the exhibit. Up ahead I heard a gasp and turned just in time to see it came from Lucy. Isabella and I hurried forward, reaching Lucy in time to see her pointing at a hieroglyphic. 'Angelique has a tattoo that looks exactly like what is painted on the woman kneeling in front of Imhotep.'"

The com sounded and the driver's voice called, "Portal in five minutes." Charlotte shivered slightly as she was pulled out of her memories. More of them were returning based on her expanded background. New perspectives were sliding into place based on the worlds she had seen and the events that had befallen her.

"What?" Anastasia looked up, startled. "Do I need to do anything for this 'Portal'?"

Charlotte focused on her slowly. "No, just be aware of it. Some people notice a strange feeling going through it and it is best if you are ready." Charlotte looked out the window to see if this was one of the approaches that would reveal the Portal in all it's glory. "Anastasia, look out the window and you can see the Portal. Do not look into it directly though. You can lose yourself in it."

The Portal loomed large and ancient ahead and to the right of them as they approached. A giant oval structure of carved and woven jet black material surrounded a swirling, pulsing field of energy. It was a combination of all colors and no color, roiling and surging in a not quite random pattern. Charlotte found it difficult to heed her own warning as she glanced at the Portal. It sang a siren song of power and understanding, promising to release its secrets if she looked just a few minutes longer.

"There is something terribly compelling about it," Anastasia commented. She was staring into it, starting to slip into a deep trance when Charlotte shook her. "I see what you mean. It promises everything for just a little more time."

"The secret is to sneak little side looks at it. Quick looks, just enough to see it without becoming too aware of it." A minute later the train swung around as the track swept in to the Portal. A shivery, shimmering, stretching rippled through them as they passed through it and into Eternia. "We are passing Portal Station, which sits just back from the Portal itself. We will pass through a long tunnel, through the Mountains of Chaos and then we will climb down into Eternia proper."

Charlotte finished speaking as they slid into the tunnel. Echoes crashed and built as the sounds of the train were amplified into a thundering wave of sound. It beat down on them, a living thing, trying to flatten them beneath its power. Charlotte reveled in it as the reminder of her new home. Anastasia clamped her hands over her ears and tried to burrow down into the bed. Anything to hide from the aweful, shattering sound.

The waves of sound poured over Anastasia, driving her thoughts back and down into the swirling fog of dim terror. In an instant the images crystallized. Dark figures swarmed into the dank cellar where Anastasia huddled with her family. Rifles were raised and a cascade of shots echoed in small room. Her parents fell instantly. Her sister and brother took a little more time. Bullets thudded and rebounded from the wealth she had sewn into her garments. In frustration one of the men smashed his rifle into her face. Bayonets slashed and tore at her and pain exploded in their wake. Her mouth opened in a silent scream as her overloaded mind finally dropped into darkness.

Wounds began to appear on Anastasia. Her body started to fade as the wounds grew more vivid and angry. "Anastasia," Charlotte called once the train cleared the tunnel. "You are safe. You are safe. Wake up, please." She shook her gently at first, then harder when she didn't respond. Her hands slid into the fading form. "Anastasia!" Charlotte shouted as she slapped Anastasia sharply.

"How dare you touch me!" Anastasia shouted. Her body snapped back to solidity and the wounds vanished. "I am the Tsar's daughter." Her face fell as she once more remembered. "They killed us. Our military imprisoned us and our people killed us." Wounds glimmered wetly on her face and neck.

"Stop. You have to stop thinking so much about your death. You are killing yourself again. Try and step back otherwise you are finished." Charlotte warned.

"It was so horrible. How could they do it to us? We were not bad people. How could we know? No one ever told me what was happening outside the palace."

"You have to not concentrate on it right now. There will be time for remembering later, after you are more settled. At the time of death it is too easy to slip back into it and die again. If you die now there is no afterlife and no coming back."

"No coming back?"

"If you die in the service of Eternia, it is final. In the Realms you can choose to go to an afterlife for the godling controlling your beliefs. Here you forfeit that option. There are exceptions. Sometimes you can choose to leave Eternia. A very few godlings will allow you into their afterlives in that case. If the one you want does not allow you in you have to appeal to a different one or choose the final death anyway. It is complicated and I do not understand all of it either. I know, for me, that I prefer the final death to any of the godlings."

"I think I understand," Anastasia answered as she stared out the window. The Mountains of Chaos rolled by, sometimes climbing high over then, sometimes dropping away as the train rushed across impossibly long and high bridges of deep chasms. Sometimes she could see the plains of Eternia in the distance, green and lush in the mid summer sun. "Maybe you could continue your story?"

"Where was I? Oh yes - Lucy and the painted image in the exhibit. Not much else was said as they continued on. The rest of the night was about what I expected. Lucy tried to explain the little bits she knew about the exhibit, but it was little more than was on the placards on the displays themselves."

"The next morning the news boys were calling out the death of a Doctor Van Helsing. He had been a respected member of the British Museum library. He was also a doctor of medicine, specializing in rare blood ailments. His body had been discovered five blocks from the museum with its heart ripped out. Before you ask, I am only reporting what I heard at the time. Now I would be curious enough to try and view the body. Then I was frightened and appalled like the rest of the populace."
