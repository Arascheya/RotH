Kali was staring out the window of the command car. The void slipped silently past her empty gaze. She raised the bottle of slivovitz she clutched in her left hand to her lips and gulped down a large swallow. The face of the first proxy victim swam in her vision, as if mirrored in the glass of the window. The scene was vivid in her mind, the horde of men and women charging at her team, the shock on the team's faces, her barked commands to attack, and the crumpling form of the woman as she had slashed her throat out. Her right hand traced where the blood had spattered her face.

"Kali?" She flinched at Petrov's voice, then turned to see him enter from the next car. "You never served in the military or saw combat, did you?" He saw her face harden and the breath she drew to speak. He cut her off with, "You are a brilliant commander and your grasp of tactics and strategy is mystical. You have saved us from so many things with your skills, but fighting others of your own kind is different. It changes you. Some people can handle it, others die because they cannot. You have to find a way to deal with it or it will consume you."

"I still see her face," Kali whispered.

Petrov moved closer to her, hesitated, then reached out and offered her his arms. She stood frozen for a long moment, then let him enfold her. "You will, for a while anyway. It will fade as you adjust."

His scent shot fire through her, bringing surging lust with it and forcing a small whimper from her before she could clamp down on herself. His eyes bored into hers for a moment, then he dropped a hand to grab her ass as he lowed his lips to hers in a hungry kiss. He broke free just long enough to whisper, "Celebrate life. It's a way to cope."
