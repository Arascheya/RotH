Last of the Sacred Units

Stylish and attractive, these Class 303 Electric Multiple Units revolutionised train services in the Glasgow area when they took over from steam in 1960. They were fast, reliable, comfortable, and had a unique feature - the passengers could see through the drivers cab and share the panoramic view of the route ahead.

This is the centre car of the three coach train, which housed all the main electrical gear, including the twenty five thousand volt pantograph pickup (on the roof at the far end in this view), and the four 207hp motors.

Sadly, this "preserved" example, 303 048, which was restored to original condition in the early 1990s, has since been scrapped, while the few remaining units in service have all been "refurbished", which is railwayspeak for having their interior ripped out and replaced by one based on the "Designed By A Committee With A Grudge" 1979 stock. This included boarding up the forward view.
