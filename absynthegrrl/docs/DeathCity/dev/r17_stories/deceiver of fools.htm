“Will this never end?”, Jerris thought as news came off the wire of yet more eastern governments falling in line with Saul McKenzie’s hard-line right-wing agenda.  Already across the eastern continents people were dying, non-conformists to the new ideas of purity and virtue being herded into ghettoes, or worse, for the crime of daring to challenge the ancient creeds.  And it was spreading.

 

Youngest and least experienced of the Guardians, Jerris found it hard to accept her fellow guardians stoicism in the face of the rising evil beyond the borders.  And then there was what she’d seen in the crystal, when, drawn almost against here will to cast her gaze eastward, she’d seen a glimpse of the force that stood behind McKenzie, huge, dark, indefatigable, ancient beyond imagining, and utterly, utterly evil.

 

Jerris slept fitfully that night.

 

*                      *                      *

 

Cold, clear, mountain air.  The brilliance of a full moon high above the pristine snow-blanketed landscape of some long-ago northern realm, a mountain kingdom, well ruled and prosperous, hard by the flanks of the mighty Northern range.  The viewpoint of an eagle, soaring through the brilliant night, swooping past mountain crags, beacon stations, and out over a broad valley where the twinkling lights of nestling farms far below dotted the silent woods and plains.

 

Onward, sweeping lower and faster, out across the plain, mountain wall on the left, rich farming valleys and long rolling hills, all blanketed in the midwinter snow, below and stretching southwards.  Onward, and here was an army, men and women on horseback, white plumes and black-and-silver standards floating on the cold night air, charging forward at breakneck speed across the snowplain to meet….

 

Ahead was only darkness.  Veiled, coiled, brooding darkness against which the moonlight failed as waves against unyielding rock.  Arround it lay the slaughtered ruin of the first army these people had sent against it, the last survivors of the rout huddled together and praying for the relief that even now swept across the plain to the rescue.  Evil stalked this fair land, and for all the glory of their charge, what hope did any of the rescuers have of even surviving their first attack, much less relieving their infantry.

 

Banking hard right and south the soaring viewpoint swept on, away from that terrible battle and onward across the kingdom, over towns where blacksmiths worked like demons to produce more and better weapons, where merchants threw open their stores to feed the defenders, and where people and priests prayed to every god they knew for help against the darkness.

 

Onward to the great capital, the city of stone built hard against the tallest and northernmost of the White Mountains, onward and upward, over guard barracks, over warehouses, over villas and mansions, up, over the great temple, up to the great citadel, and into a lofty chamber.

 

Here was Margred, seer of her people, witch, sorceress, protector.  Advisor to the king, speaker to the realm beyond, for more than 50 years she’d warded the borders with magic and kept the people from harm, yet against this invading force of darkness she was powerless.

 

However valiant the warriors, when the coils of the darkness brushed over them their courage failed and their strength departed, leaving them weak and defenceless to be butchered by the dark army that marched within that evil shroud.  Her own magicks could not penetrate the veils, for all that she hurled herself and her powers into the task.

 

And now she stood by her chamber’s open window, gazing north to where even now the king sent another force in desperate peril to try and fight, all the while the invaders moved on, ever closer to the capital, leaving naught but butchery and ruin in their wake.

 

Magred had failed.  Her powers exhausted, her body close to death, all her arts had failed utterly against that impenetrable cloak of evil.  Still her people rallied to the defence of their land, but she knew despair.

 

And yet, was there perhaps still one desperate hope to try?

 

Beyond the chamber a walkway extended out to the scrying point, a circular platform built out from the mountain face, fully a thousand feet above the plain below, a place of peril and high magick.  Seizing her sacred blade in one hand, Margred summoned the last of her strength and walked slowly, but steadily, out of the chamber, along the narrow way, and stepped to the very edge of the scrying platform. Facing north, toward the advancing evil, with both hands she grasped the hilt and plunged the blade deep into her own heart, stepping off the platform into space as she did so.

 

Down plummeted her dying body, but out and up roared her soul, gossamer wings riding the air, growing, soaring, and speeding north to meet the foe.  There, below her, was the king’s third army, lead by his own son and daughter.  There ahead, almost consumed by the evil dark, were the survivors of the first two forces, huddled and desperate for succour.  And then Margred was amongst them and her soaring spirit, given new power past imagining by her own sacrifice, sang and called to each and every one, blowing away the darkness, replacing stolen strength, and shielding their minds with warnings of the true nature of what they faced.

 

“Remember who you are, what you stand for”, she sang in their minds, “he can only be if you believe what he tells you”, all the while warding, guarding, and summoning the power of the spirits of the land itself to lend them the strength to fight on.

 

The king’s army charged into the darkness like a thunderclap of the gods, and this time it was the dark forces who felt the terror of unstoppable butchery.  Clean through their ranks the prince and princess rode, laying waste to all around them in a frenzy of flashing steel and slaughter.  Behind came their army, reinforced by the survivors who no longer huddled but instead roared back to the attack, wielding all and any weapons they could muster and utterly routing their erstwhile attackers.

 

In minutes it was over, the physical lord of the dark forces slain, his army routed, and the great evil power they had summoned fled with unimaginable speed to whatever dark lair it had come from, banished, driven out by Magred’s final strike.

 

*                      *                      *

 

Jerris jerked awake, thrown physically out of bed by the force of the dream.  Or was it a dream?  There in the chamber air in front of her floated Magred’s face, her expression an unreadable mix of joy and sorrow.  She said but one sentence, and then she was gone.

 

“Shall he rule, again?”
