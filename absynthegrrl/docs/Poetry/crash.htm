Crash - written in 1978

I stand at the window,

I see the blackness complete;

Enclosing my world as a blanket,

Strangling her life,

Crippling her with death.


I walk through the shops.

Wares still hang there,

Wares waiting, waiting for hands

That have gone.

Everyone has gone.

When next I see the shops

The wares will be dust.

Memories, rust, thought, and dust.


I see the houses.

Empty, silent, unlit,

They stare accusingly.

I hear the ghost echoes of children:

See the people/shadows drifting, driftiing...

The darkness deadens the echoes,

The shadows dissolve,

I see only houses...


Once again I see the garden,

Shrouded in black

Not even the moons shine.

No shadows, no people, no light,

Emptiness all.


I see the plain bloodied.

Strewn with bits of flesh and metal.

Bits of individuals entangled in one.

A once free civilization lies running together,

Mingling with the dust.

Wreckage glowing in the night.

The sun begins to rise.


I look and see her.

She is looking down at me.

There is blood on her robe,

My blood,

And tears, her tears.

This day was to be ours.

This day we were to be free.

We were to start a new life.


Now there is only the ticking,

Metal cooling.

Blood, too much blood.

The ground and wreckage covered with it.

Our day of newness turned to ash,

Turned to blood.


Survivors, shocked, cling to loved ones.

She meets my eyes,

"We will survive," she says

"We will remember and return!"

All is not lost.

Some will survive.
