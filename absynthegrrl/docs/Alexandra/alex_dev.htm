Consider this section on religion - could come out while talking to Karyne and Tori during a camp or in transit. Could be prompted by a Mayfly that comes from a religious background, or is talking about it

The universe is far more of a miracle if it evolved by itself. Having something create it reduces the wonder. Any intelligence can concoct something, but random evolution of life is fucking amazing.

Humans are one of the better arguments against creation or intelligent design. Many of the flaws, oddities, and poorly designed systems make a lot more sense in a random evolution model.

Relying on some cosmic force to solve problems and force people to be good cheapens the beauty of kindnesses performed for their own sake. Which is more sincere, an act of true kindness or an act of kindess done to curry favor or out of fear of reprisal?

The tough love of any human tends to be more humane and caring than the "love" exhibited by the sky gods of the dominant religions. If "imperfect beings" can manage love, why does a "god" have to be such an ass about it?

It is a truly sad commentary on humanity that it takes coersion and fear of some fantasy being and fantasy place of torment to promote good behavior. It is also a sad commentary that it is easier to rely on a fantasy being for good things in life than it is to rely on each other.

The whole concept of terrible things happening as a "test" or "growth promoting experience" is just sick. Better to explain it as a random act of an unthinking universe, it makes it far less personal and targetted that way.
