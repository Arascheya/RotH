Additional Data and Thoughts for Prelude to a Dream

Ennui stepped off the bus in downtown. Immediately the memories overwhelmed her. Even before her parents saw her she began to regret this trip. "Anna, over here! Oh my god, what have you done to your hair?" Her mother waved and yelled. For the first time she wished she'd managed to get all the color washed out before coming home. The stares on the bus were fun, but the lecture she was sure to get now wouldn't be.

"Mama, da, hi. Where's Cindy?"

"The school dance. Seems she has some of your habits, although she is more studious. I still can't believe you're wasting your time on theater instead of medicine."

"I don't want to be a nurse. I don't cope well with sick people."

"But there's no future in theater. Your mother figured that out when she left the circus."

"Da, that's not true. The circus had to add a second unit to handle all the requests for shows and smaller circuses are starting up everywhere." She stared back into his angry look. "I am glad the two of you got together, and I love Cindy too. It's just I miss my friends. I miss the thrill of the show. Most of all I miss the travel. Can't you understand? Not everyone is meant to settle down."

"Anna, I had my time being wild with the circus and I outgrew it. I don't want you to have to go through the same things I did."

"Mama, what did you go through? We had friends and I was learning things besides the boring stuff they crammed at me in the schools here."

"Now don't you start. We tried to give you a good life..."

"And you did. You really did in a lot of ways. I just don't want to settle down. I see some of the others at university already giving away their options chasing security. I understand the desire, but the cost seems way too high!"

"Now my dear, how many times have I tried to tell you that life isn't always about what you want."

"I know da, but can't it be once in a while?"

"Now Anna, we've had these conversations every time you come home. Could we just leave it for now?" Her mom pleaded.

"Ok mama, let's go home. I'd like to wash the trip off and get one of your good meals as well."
