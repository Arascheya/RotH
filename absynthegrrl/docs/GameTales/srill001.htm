After my mom's death father was off in the city more often than he was at home. We spent our time with Philip and Marya, one of the neighbors, and their kids. They agreed to watch over us as a favor to the memory of my mom. We were three additional mouths they had to feed, but I never heard them complain. Philip was always busy around town, mending this, or fixing that while Marya would make simple herb preparations and do sewing. We ran wild through the town with their brood, returning to eat and sleep. In many ways the entire village became our family, watching over the herd of us.

I've always been more curious than is healthy, so when I found a locked door open I was through it immediately.  I was midway through the room, examining a suit of armor that told me stout, staid Marya hadn't always been so dull, when Philip leapt through the door brandishing sword and dagger. You know, Koriana, if you are going to snoop into things the very least you should do is close the door behind you." After a few more chuckles he continued," Now, just out of curiosity, what did you see and what has it told you?"

He confronted her one night and offered her an alternative. She could leave the city with him and he wouldn't turn her in. Apparently the arrangement was agreeable since they have been here ever since.

Then he gave me some of the best advice I have ever gotten.  "Always look beneath the surface, look for the hidden agenda, the real reasons for an action.
