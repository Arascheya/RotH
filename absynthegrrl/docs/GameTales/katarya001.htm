Quest for Irena Katarya - Chapter 1

Alyssa stood in the door of the inn, looking out over the same dusty track she had seen for her eighteen summers. To her right it faded away into the deeper forest of the upper valley where a few trappers and maybe a bandit or two lived. To her left it slipped through the tiny village of Stratmore-Upon-Comcrash, before winding down to the city of Miznyierash. The sun hung bloody over the tips of the Barrier Mountains and a hint of fall chill touched the air. A loud thunder-like boom sounded from near the village watch tower. In the late night air a huge gleaming spider had appeared just beyond the village. Men in black and orange swarmed down a ramp that dropped from its head. Shouted orders echoed around them. "Kill the men, gather the women and children."

Lightning leapt from the strange weapons in their hands, making quick work of any that tried to oppose them. The sun had barely moved before the village women were rounded up and shoved into a huddled group at the foot of the ramp. The soldiers fell silent as a tall, blonde haired man stepped onto the ramp. At his side was a wizened old woman, stooped and leaning heavily on his arm. The woman raised a shaky hand and pointed at Alyssa and her mother. "Those two, dread lord. Together they are the key to crystal. Apart, they are nothing."

"And of the other?" The voice was cultured and cold, sending chills down my spine.

"Kaltereth is not here, dread lord." The woman cowered slightly as she said this.

"No matter, soon she shall be found. Kill the rest, burn the village, and bring these two to the guest chambers. They will tell me where crystal is very soon." He turned and helped the old woman back into the structure. Lightning flashed, death screams, and the smell of burned flesh followed Alyssa and her mother as they were shoved roughly up the stairs.

At the top of the stairs the world shifted and blurred into an enormous throne room. The walls and floor were gold veined black marble, clear crystalline pillars supported the arching ceiling. Worked into its surface was blackness with a swirling spiral armed galaxy floating in it. At the end of the chamber a throne of fossilized bone perched on a five foot high dais. As Alyssa drew closer she could make out the shape of a dragon in the bones with the seat and backrest being formed of the pelvis, spine, and ribs. Various other bones formed the arm rests and the skull arched overhead, staring down at her as she approached. Two carven chairs flanked the throne on their own platforms three feet up from the main hall. One appeared to be deep green emerald and the other ruby.

Three figures sat in the chairs, sprawled in various forms of relaxation, and talking animatedly among themselves. They did not notice Alyssa as she crept closer. She stopped at the foot of the stairs to the dais. The woman in the bone throne noticed her and motioned her up to the platform. "Dreamwalker, welcome."

At the top of the stairs the world spun and shifted to a ruined courtyard. Three figures sat on shattered columns and rubble around a large cauldron and fire. One sat in the shadows, face covered in darkness and a deep hood, one sat close to the fire, her face illuminated in flickering gold, and the third sat half in the shadows. This one motioned to Alyssa. "Come here girl. Warm yourself by our fire." Her voice was warm and inviting.

"Be at peace girl. This is your dream. You are safe enough." The hooded figure whispered in a deeper echoing voice. Slowly Alyssa crossed to the fire.

"Who..who are you? Why are you in my dreams, and what do you want?"

"I am Katya," the partially shadowed figure answered. "And these are my two sisters, Maranashma," she motioned to the hooded figure. "and Cenefalys." she motioned to the fully lit woman. "And you are?"

"I am Alyssa. What do you want?"

"We want nothing. We were having a quiet time together when you summoned us with your terror." Cenefalys answered.

"You have the gift I see. Like your grandmother before you." Katya added.

"Gift? Nightmares are a gift now? And what was that...thing...?"

"They are more than dreams, dreamwalker, as you well know. You serve yourself ill with this ridiculous deception," Katya warned.

"What did you see? We cannot read your mind, young one." Maranashma whispered.

"A giant spider disgorging men, killing my village."

"The Perverter of Spiders. A powerful being, that. Beware of him. He will do you no good." Cenefalys replied. "Do you know what he wanted?"

"No, although he was going on about some crystal when he ordered my mother and I seized."

"Are you sure he said 'a crystal'?"

"I think it was 'crystal'..."

"It is as we feared." Maranashma whispered.

"He seeks the warrior woman, and by extension, her companions." Katya continued.

"You are in danger young one. You, your village, and any who know you."Cenefalys added.

"Easy sister, you are scaring her. She is newly arrived at this level of Vision. But she is right Alyssa, there is great danger coming. Examine your Vision carefully, look for any clues of how this will unfold. That is the key to understanding and altering events," Katya soothed.

"It can be changed?" Alyssa asked.

"Of course. No future is set until it has unfolded, although some are more determined than others. The Perverter of Spiders is quite determined so events involving this being will be harder to shift, but it is still possible."

"What does this Perverter of Spiders want? Who is this warrior women and what does any of this have to do with me?"

"The Perverter wants a book the warrior woman, called Krystal, has seen. To that end he seeks her through her companion, Irena Katarya." Katya replied.

"Irena? What do you know of Irena? Please, tell me!"

"And why is this important to you?" Katya asked in return.

"Irena was from my village. She's one of the few who escaped and the only one to become an adventuress of great renown. I want to find her."

"You obsess over her, don't you?" Katya added. "Why is that?"

"All my life I've heard the tales of her. How she left my village and went off to find greatness."

"And you believe adventuring makes you great? Might not greatness be found in other ways?" Maranashma asked.

"I have no idea. I only know I am dying here. Soon I'll be expected to marry and then have children. I want more than that."

"But how do you know it is different anywhere else? People live, love, have children, and work to support each other. That is the way of things in most of the world. Hell, in most of the Multi-Verse." Katya added.

"But it's not what I want. There has to be more to life than that. If that's all there is maybe I should end myself and try the next phase of existence."

"Suicide is always an option. Yet, what if the next phase is similar? What if most of the next realm is day to day existence as well? Have you considered that?" Katya replied.

"Maybe it is. I just know I have to get out of here. I have to see if there is more somewhere else. Surely the entire world can't be as dull as here."

"Maybe not, but what will you do if it is?" Katya asked.

"I can still find Irena and talk to her. She'd talk to someone from her village, I know she would."

"And if she's dead? What then?" Katya asked in return.

"I continue adventuring and strive to become like her."

"Are you sure you know her well enough to do that? What if she isn't this "hero" you think she is?" Cenefalys asked.

"She is."

"You can't know that. What if she has done monstrous things? Become a terrible being?" Maranashma asked from the shadows.

"And if she has? I won't know until I find her. Or find someone who knew her."

"If you are that determined there are two people you should try and find. They were friends to Irena," Katya said.

"And they are?"

"The first is Koria Dzahmahth, a demoness that became her friend," Cenefalys answered.

"A demoness? Surely that can't be right..."

"Ah, the certainty of youth. Do not be so certain of your knowledge, young madam. There may be many legends about Irena, the greatest of the Rogues and Shadowdancers, yet there is little real knowledge of her left," Katya warned.

"And the other, then?"

"Darina Snowden, a resident of Stone Leigh, set at the doors to Ravenloft. Darina was her second friend," Maranashma replied.

"Where should I find these two?"

"At last you begin to understand. In the tales of the bards the adventurers already know the path and they are walking it. In reality, they may spend most of their lives seeking the adventure they crave. But you have heard of Irena from someone, maybe they know of her friends as well," Maranashma said.

"Everyone in town knows of Irena. No one knows anything about where she is though."

"There is the path of research and sages, the path of digging for the lost knowledge to find a thread to follow." Cenefalys added.

"True..."

"Or there is the heading off into the unknown and hoping that somewhere else you may find a crumb or a clue to that which you seek. All of these are options for you to pursue. Although, you could ask your mother about Irena," Katya mentioned.

"And why should I do that?"

"Have you ever talked to your mother about her life? It might surprise you. She wasn't always your mother and she wasn't always in this village," Katya asked.

"She never talks about herself. I tried when I was little but she always deferred to my father. She left for a few years and then came back. That's all she's ever said."

"And you've never wondered if there was a reason she did that? Maybe you should ask her again rather than assume you know her. Give her a chance before you leave. She might surprise you," Katya said.

"I guess maybe I should."


Early morning sunlight slipped down the wall and into Alyssa's eyes. Slowly she opened them, the last wisps of the dream remaining as a film across the room for a moment more. She rolled out of bed and dressed for the day. "Mom, mom, can I talk to you for a few minutes?" she called as she thundered down stairs and into the kitchen.

"Later, right now you're late for your duties as server. Now, grab those bowls and get them out to tables two three and eight. You know we're serving breakfast for the next hour." Alyssa grabbed the bowls and headed out into the common room. Her father held court behind the bar, dispensing early morning drinks and keeping old Elsias from falling off his stool. The rest of the village morning regulars were in their various places as well, some at the tables scattered around the room and a few tucked up to the bar.

"Morning Alyssa. Happy birthday," Roweana said as Alyssa slid her bowl a bowl in front of her. "Given any thought to my offer of a spot at the barracks? Your tracking skills make you a good catch."

"Still thinking about it, Row."

Old Lorin barely grunted as she placed his bowl near his right and leaned down to kiss his head. "Morning grandfather."

"Eh, oh, happy birthday Lyssa. How has your day been so far?"

"I overslept again, other than that, good." She moved around the table to hug her grandmother, "Morning Gran."

"Morning dear." Alyssa started to place the last bowl in front of her grandmother when the main door opened and three large men in black and orange garb, exactly matching the uniforms the spider men had worn in her dreams, walked into the inn. The one in front crossed to her father at the bar while the other two headed for table ten. Some of the food slopped out as her hand started shaking. "Alyssa, go into the kitchen, quickly," her grandmother whispered sharply. She turned and walked rapidly back out of the main room.

"Any more orders, honey?"

"No mom, just some strangers talking to dad."

"Traders?"

"I don't know. They're wearing military style gear in blacks and oranges."

"With black web and spider patches on the sleeves?"

"Yeah, how did you know?"

"Tersa mentioned seeing a group dressed that way in Miznyierash last time she came through. Said they were polite enough unless they wanted something, then they were pure trouble. Apparently they are even more numerous down river, toward Frenlayish and Azkelon on the great central lake."

"Who are they allied with?"

"Tersa didn't know for certain. Some new noble from an old family returning to ancestral lands was the common rumor. Did you check if they wanted anything?"

"Gran sent me back here."

"Guess your father will have to take care of it then. You wanted to ask me something earlier?"

"I wanted to ask you about when you left the village, and about Irena..."

"Lyss, I don't..."

"Mom, I had a dream, about the village and men like those who just came in. I'm scared, they killed everyone except for you and me."

"Honey, it's just a dream."

"No, no it's not. I...I've had them before, just like gran, and things happen to people, just like in them. Oh gods, mom, I'm scared!"

"Lyss, why didn't you tell us? Gran could have helped you!" Her mother pulled her into a close hug.

"I thought they'd go away. They never amounted to much before. I saw Tevin cut himself, or Laura sprain her ankle, but never anything like this. These men, they want Irena, and their master thinks we can give her to him."

Her mom sighed heavily, "Go wake your sister, she'll need to cover for you. Have her send gran back in, she can cook for a while, won't kill her yet I suppose. Then I'll tell you about it."

Alyssa ran up the stairs and skidded into the room she shared with her sister. "Anna, wake up, mom needs you," she called at the blonde head, three quarters buried under her blankets, on the bottom bunk.

"Go away, pest," The sleepy head mumbled as it turned toward the wall.

"Seriously, mom needs you.Come on, you got to get up!"

Blazing blue eyes stared out of a sleepy face as Anna rolled back over. "Damn it! You kept me awake half the night with your tossing and whimpering and now I have to get up? It's not right."

"I'm sorry Anna."

"Yeah, Yeah, give me a minute." Anna swung her long legs off the top bunk and shuddered at the cold wood of the floor. She pulled a long dress over her sleep tunic and staggered toward the stairs. "Well, you coming?"

"Anna, you need to take Lyssa's shift."

"Why, are her legs broken?" Anna whined.

"Gran Saw something, something bad, and Lyss needs to keep out of sight. Now go check on the common room and ask gran to come back here."

"Oh, oh damn, sorry Lyss!" Anna was suddenly wide awake and focused. She took a moment to flip her hair into place then hurried through the door.

Gran slipped into the kitchen before the door had a chance to close. "Yes, I can cook while you and Lyss talk. Anna and I can take care of it, you two go and talk. And Luci, might I recommend the forest patch where the good mushrooms grow?" Alyssa and her mother hurried out the back and up the track into the deeper forest.

Five minutes quick walking brought them to a faint trail off to the left. Soon they were settled on dry moss and spongy needles under and ancient pine. For a long moment her mother stared out into the small clearing beyond. "I don't like to talk about that time in my life..."

"But mother..."

"Hear me out. I don't like to talk about it because it was wonderful and horrible, and the horrible bits took years to forget," She stared up into the canopy of the tree over them and then began, "I was your age when it all started. Like you I was bored and restless, slipping away from the inn whenever I could to walk in the woods, slide into the barns with one cute boy or the other, normal stuff I guess. The only thing that kept me out of trouble was the women's wine gran gave me. One hot summer night I was on my way back to the inn, rather later than usual, and not looking forward to gran's switch. I had stopped for a moment to look up at the stars when a brilliant streak of light flashed across the sky from down river and toward the icy wastes of the Barrier Mountains."

"People said lights in the sky were omens, at the time I prayed it meant I would soon get out of Stratmore. Others say 'Beware what you ask of the gods, for they may grant it'. Those people are very right!"

"I returned to the inn, got the switching I deserved, and went to bed, thinking little more on it. The next day passed without event and the feeling of change started to fade. By dusk I was ready to believe I had imagined it until I stepped out to cool down from the heat of the kitchen. Shadows moved on the road, the road we just came down. At first I thought it was bandits. This was before the Hell Gate Wars and there were more bandits in our woods then. I was going to slip back inside when I got a better look. Two figures were leaning on each other and staggering down the road. It could have been a trick, but I resolved to watch a bit longer."

"As they drew closer I could see it was two women, leaning on each other for support. One was rail thin and wiry. The other was dark skinned with the look of a soldier or swordswoman. Her face marked her as from the Lotus Empire as did her speech when she spoke, "We seek a healer. I have friends up the road, we were attacked by bandits." I looked at them closely in the light from the door. The thin woman was supporting the dark one. Her eyes watched everything with a predator's vigilance. The dark one looked drained and grey under her dark skin. They both had fine woolen blankets pulled close around themselves even though the night was warm."

"I am shamed to say I hesitated for a very long time. It could have so easily been a trap. It wasn't until the dark one turned to walk into the village that I thawed and replied, "I am trained in healing. You may rest here while my companion takes me back to your friends."

"I am afraid I am the only one that can understand your speech. I must return with you." They turned and lead the way back up the road to a place a little farther up the valley from here. A small pile of bodies brought me to a quick halt. "Over here, please," the dark one motioned. Just off the road were five others. Three of them had deep sword cuts, one, a winged creature of some unknown origin, had arrows in 'her' wings and chest. The last one sat against a tree mumbling to herself in a foreign language. They were all dressed in strange fabrics and odd patterns. Everything about them said strange, foreign, unknown except for the blood oozing from their wounds and the look of fatigue and pain on their faces."

"I started with the most seriously injured, a man named Will. Then moved on to a woman named Kara, and then to a small, delicate woman named Lynn. Kyira was the winged one, she was called a Vamyr by the dark one. The dark one gave her name as Krystal and the name of the thin woman as Shandi."

"Krystal?" Alyssa cried. "She is the one the black and orange leader wants."

"Oh no. This is not good at all."

"What is it, mom?"

"It's in the tale. Soon you will know. The final woman was Irena, but something had happened to her that I couldn't fix. I tried all the head related healing I knew but she kept rocking and mumbling. In desperation I asked Krystal to translate. She looked at me and finally replied, 'She's saying the same thing over and over again, 'My name is Irena Katarya, I have red hair.' She should have come out of this by now, but she hasn't and Kara and I don't know how to help. Could we bring her to some place in town? And maybe find a barn or something to rest in?'

"'You can stay at our inn. I'm sure my mother would let you stay a night for nothing since you were attacked.' I had them follow me back and we settled them in the inn. Gran wasn't very happy about it at first but it got better when they started helping around the inn. As days turned into months they slowly learned enough to speak to us and began to settle in. Irena's recovery was the slowest of all. Among themselves they spoke of 'cyber crash' and 'brain burn'. Her actions and responses to the world around her were those of a toddler just learning how to function on her own."

"They were always a bit odd though. Most of them had to learn even the simplest animal or farming tasks. Only Krystal and Shandi seemed familiar with weapons. Evenings often found them sitting at the farthest corner table talking among themselves in the strange language I had first heard or talking silently with finger talking."

"And Shandi, she was scary. She could barely string words together yet she watched everything, and her reflexes were faster than anything I have ever seen. I saw her fight once when a bold group of bandits decided to try and raid the inn. Shandi killed most of with short daggers and her feet before most of us had time to grab weapons. And she seemed to love every minute of it, attacking with a savage joy that was terrifying to behold."
