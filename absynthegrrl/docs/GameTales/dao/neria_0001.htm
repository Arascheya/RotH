Neria

"Did you pick up anything important from the repository?"

"No, there was nothing among the trinkets sufficiently interesting."

"Then where did you get that, that indecent bit of cloth and metal? Why is my initiate derssed in something similar?" Greagoir demanded.

"You think we wanted to die down there? First Enchanter, I really hope the guardians were constructs rather than people. We did eliminate quite a few."

"But where did you get it? Tell me!"

"Greagoir, I know you mean well, but back off. Your right to interfere with me has ended. You think demonic possession is the worst thing in the multi-verse? You are quite mistaken. Even this blight it but a burp in the functionings of the whole."

"What are you and what have you done with Neria?" Irving asked.

"There was something interesting, but it wasn't in the repository. I found a ring in the store rooms. This ring..."

Irving bent to look, then backed away, face ashen, "That ring was in a warded chest, dropped into the lake. How did you find it?"

"I was helping inventory the store romm and found it on the floor. Next thing I remember it was on my finger, I was wearing this, and several hours had passed. The oil in my lantern was nearly gone."

"Guards..." Greagoir began.

"Greagoir," Neria spoke in a velvet voice with steel buried within. "You are a good man, but the misguided beliefs of the Chantry won't save you. Not reacting foolishly or in a threatening way is the only way you will survive. First Enchanter, perhaps you'd like to fill him in?"
