Sea Voyage

I had been on the royal barges and various boats on the Nile but nothing prepared me for the smoking behemoth that greeted me at the docks when I began my journey to London. We were herded in and expertly shuffled into our rooms. I had taken mid-cost passage. Expensive enough to be comfortable but not so much as to draw unwanted attention.

The Mediterranean was wonderful. The sun was warm and the ship slid gracefully through the small waves around it. We stopped at several ports however I remained on the ship. I was confused enough just trying to sort out all the data I was getting from sampling other passengers.

When we reached the Atlantic the weather changed from the warm sunlight I had known all my life into a wild raging thing. Water fell in sheets from the sky. Cold winds buffeted the ship. As we moved toward the north the temperature dropped and a damp chill always seemed to invade my being. I mostly stayed in my cabin, only venturing out to take a random sample once in a while. I kept the coal fire in my stove running as hot as I could to try and stay warm. It didn't help very much.
