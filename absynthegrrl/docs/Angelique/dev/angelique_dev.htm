London, what can I say that will convey my feelings of this place? I loved it and I hated it. There was a throbbing pulse of activity and intensity that was fascinating, yet there was smoke and noise, stench and darkness. The people were dirty, prudish, and repressed. The station of women was an abomination. From the first day I stepped from the ship I longed to return to the brilliant sun and clear skies of Mennufer. The samples I had taken on the voyage had given me the basics of the society, yet I could scarcely bring myself to adopt them. To be a proper woman I was to dress myself in constrictive clothing, walk about as if ashamed of myself, and defer to any man I encountered, regardless of station.

Still, the visions promised a Door or a way to find the place of my mother. I could not refuse to follow it. So it was with reluctance and despirited acceptance that I began my stay in the belching, wheezing, and clanking metropolis of London.

I long for the brilliant sun. For the golden light on the monuments. the sound of instruments and children on the wind, the smells of cooking fires drifting through the evening on a cleaner wind. This damp, this dank, dirty and dark grey light is blinding and depressing. Where are the blue skies and greens of life? Here it is all greyness and dark. What life there is seems stunted and twisted from living in the darkness and breathing vapours of death.
