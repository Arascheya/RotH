Kara is half Eritsani from her mother's side. She is the heir to Dracon Pharaceuticals as well.

Kara had a relationship going with a planet bound lover, she wanted Kara to settle down, Kara wanted her to join her on the ghost wind. they couldn’t come to any sort of agreement and argued about it incessantly until it finally broke them up, this was about a year before Valen arrived on the scene - right now Kara is enjoying her autonomy - she will explain about this if asked but leave the gender ambiguous

Kara has lived on ghost wind for 5 years sometimes with lovers, sometimes by herself. She has some bad habits that way, such as drinking/eating direstly from containers and not needing to dress to move around the ship. She has mostly reverted to Eritsani type - a ship, with its known boundaries is far more comforting than the open, unbound space of a planet.

most of the time when she is alone on ghost wind she will turn the gravity off and rarely wear much of anything. Once each day she’ll turn the gravity on to 1.5 normal and exercise in the gym room. She rarely hauls passengers, and if she does, she rents self contained passenger modules and installs them in the cargo section and will not actually link them into the living spaces of ghost wind - she will always charge triple rate for passenger runs

Ghost Wind need cabins for Kara/Valen, Lynn, will, Krys corridors are stripped of coverings, with only pads where they are needed for maneuvering with the gravity off. The ship started life as one of the early DSC designs, right as they started thinking about a separable living/control module. Kara retrofitted ghost wind for this to work, increased the drives and power plant, added weapons systems, and in general made many modifications to the ship. She holds clear title to it.

her mother has almost equal influence with her father since half the money for acquiring and setting up Drakon came from her family on Eritsan. She is a highly placed member of one of the powerful Eritsani clans and this has allowed Drakon access to the imperial zones under their influence, and a substantial edge over their competitors. She is still a strong believer in the Eritsani deities and universe views. She brought Kara up as a devote of Eritsa, the goddess of change

having gotten money through her mom for original purchase and then financing modifications in whatever ways she could.

When they arrived the ship was empty except for a very scared, almost dead kitten hiding in the galley. There was a crew/passenger module loaded into the cargo bay that was empty and blown to space. There were signs of weapons fire and blood in places but no bodies, and no sizable patches. She got money through her mom to buy the ship from the salvage yard who was going to break it down and sell it in pieces since few would buy a ship where the crew vanished. /* on evolution, children, and grounders */

Our species evolved in a hostile environment, so those that were best at assessing threats were the survivors. This took place over a very long time and a large number of generations. As a result, we are still wired for threat assessment, however, when a safe environment comes along, there are no real threats. As a result, threats are seen where there are none, or minimal ones at best.

The same happened with child rearing. Those that took the best care of their children survived. Now, without real threats to their ofspring, they percieve threats and dangers completely out of proportion to the reality.

Grounders have been able to create extremely safe environments, but it is making them crazy. For every safety advance, there is an even stronger need for more, driving safety into the realm of unhealthy obsession.

The Eritsani have reversed some of this, slowly, over the length of our time in the void. Those who could sacrifice their children survived. Those that sacrificed themselves for their children doomed both. As a result, we can view children as both precious and as a renewable resource. If the adults survive, there can always be more children.

/* on simplicity */

life was simple. Run this ship, carry cargo to make enough to meet expenses, and go pretty much where ever there was cargo. Then along comes this complication. The butcher drops in on my ship and turns my whole universe upside down. Not only is there another mouth to feed, there’s also someone with their own agenda. Could it be someone with simple needs? No, it has to be someone on the run from very serious things.

I like simplicity, that’s why I run this ship. I only have to keep the ship maintained and stocked. No worries about additional crew, a ground place, an office, or anything else. Then you come along and complicate it all up. Now there’s another person to worry about. More supplies to be bought, another person’s feelings and needs to take into account. And could I get someone simple? No, I got you. the butcher, the premiere, and a strong woman besides. What am I supposed to do with you? I tried to off-load you at the nearest port, but that didn’t work. Then I tried to leave you with your friend and that didn’t work either. Now Jones is attached to you and so am i.
