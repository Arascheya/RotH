On Energy

IRENA: The most critical task facing any technology group is a sound energy policy. I have seen many worlds stand at the edge of greatness, only to be crippled because they ran out of cheap energy. Make sure you diversify your energy sources as completely as you can and always be leading the curve on new sources. No source of energy lasts forever.

Kara - about security service

VALEN: I left because I got tired of the compartmentalised lies. No one had the full story of anything. More missions were about political infighting and power brokering than security of the alliance. Once I pieced that together, I could no longer respect my bosses or do their dirty errands. I finally broke when they told me to kill a civilian for the simple fact of finding something inconvenient to the beliefs of one member of the assembly. I tried to reduce that in the Empire security group, but I don't think that was very successful either. People drawn to clandestine affairs love the game of it, no matter how pointless, dirty, or self-serving. It's like power, if a person wants to do it, they shouldn't be allowed to.
