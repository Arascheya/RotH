"Far away, not that it's any of your business. Now leave!"

"No, you owe me. Torr was my lover and you killed him. You at least owe me for that."

"Owe you!?" I couldn't believe it, who did she think she was anyway. "I owe you nothing. Now, get off my ship."

"No."

"Then I'll have you removed."

"And you'll never learn what I knew of your son, the things he'd never dream of telling you." Curiosity stopped me. She saw my hesitation and pressed her advantage. "Take me away from here and I'll tell you of the Torr I knew, then you can put me down anywhere you like and be quit of me."

"As you wish then." I turned to the computer and issued the voice identity sequence, specified a course to Berzan, and initiated launch.

"There are several staterooms on this ship to choose from. Pick one and settle in, it'll be a long trip." So saying, I turned and headed toward mine, ignoring Solina.

Later, as I headed to the galley for a snack, I passed Torr's suite and found the door open. Inside Solina was wandering aimlessly, touching Torr's few items. I started to enter then changed my mind and continued on. She wandered in a few minutes later. "Why are you taking us to Berzan?"

"I don't see how it should matter to you."

"Why are you running, Servalan? What do you hope to find? Or what are you trying to hide from?"

"Nothing, not that it's any of your business, now leave me alone."

"I'm carrying his child." Her words dropped me to the deck, as deep inside something started screaming. Images cascaded through my head, Torr as a baby, as a young boy, then blood and silence. Curled tight, whimpering, I huddled in the corner. Frantically I pushed further back as Solina moved toward me. "No, stay away from me! Don't touch me!" By the time she was within five feet of me I was screaming incoherently at her, but still she kept coming. Her touch was sandpaper on an open burn. Her arms as they wrapped around me were steel bands. Still screaming I retreated back into my mind, closing doors as I dove deeper, until I felt nothing, remembered nothing, and saw nothing.
