/* on religious convictions */

Kara - Do you believe in anything outside yourself then?

Valen - Not in the way you mean. She gestured to the plants around them - there is the true mystery of the universe, right there - life...

Valen - The rest is each race trying to create something bigger than themselves, but still like them - I don’t think the ‘deity’ of the universe is anything like that - it is life itself, trying to find an understanding of itself, trying to keep itself going...the rest is our vanities asserting themselves. Who am I to claim I speak for ‘the universe’ that my words are the ‘truth’ of everything? What arrogance, what self deception.

Valen - We get hooked into needing to accomplish things. I wanted to form the Alliance into something better than before. I wanted to make it stable and kind and all sorts of things yet everything I tried was opposed at every step. On one hand we seek excitement and experience, yet we deeply resist change. Yet our very existence is constant change - we are born, we grow, we age... what if the importance isn’t our accomplishments but the changes we go through themselves? What if our resistance to change is what allows us to perceive it? What if we are an attempt by the universe itself to experience itself? Maybe all those sermons on bettering ourselves and being productive are all a smoke screen over the truth - a truth that is far more indifferent than we want it to be.

Kara - Are you sure you weren’t raised Eritsani? Those are very Eritsani concepts. We are, the universe is. Our purpose is to experience the universe. We have ethics we should follow, ethics essential to the survival of a group of individuals, but many of the "truths" of the Grounders are not valid for us. These are the truths of the Eritsani. Some of us worship the Goddess of Changes, but she is more a focusing of this concept rather than a set apart diety.

The trick is to remember that it is very hard to create and guide a situation to a result you desire. Every being will surprise you. Say you’ve watched your target for weeks and every morning she goes to this particular café at the same time. On the day you decide to make your move she will over-sleep, or be sick, or be entertaining someone. Something will be different and the plan will have to change.

As my fingers danced over the keyboard, rapidly entering security cracking commands and codes I wasn’t noticing the intricacies I would have expected. The final two levels to reach birth and personnel records were ridiculously easy. The changes were easy to insert and soon the system was creating an identi-card and record updates for Irina Katarya. Setting up the routine to update the records at Imperial Registry was a little more challenging since it had to add the records, back date them to the right time frame, then erase itself from transmission and monitoring logs. As I backed out of the system I invoked part of the security check system and scanned for enabled security mechanisms. About half of the standard routines had been shut off. Whoever had been in here was adept, but very careless about removing tracks. Playing a guess I diverted and ran one of the basic system utilities to check recently deleted files and virtual memory use. Then a quick call to a binary code editor revealed what I had guessed. Fragments of a distinctive security cracking utility remained in several locations. It was an old one I hadn’t seen since leaving the intelligence group, effective, but common and dated.

Kara watched fascinated as screen after screen of odd symbols flashed across the screen as I barely even seemed to glance at them. She started to ask a question or two until I barely shook my head and wandered my eyes around the small area. Catching on almost immediately she launched into questions about the ‘problem’ we were supposed to be fixing and how much longer it was going to take.

"There’s one more thing I need to check here - some of the secondary lines seem to be damaged also. Probably another half hour." Quickly scanning the file fragments remaining revealed extensive copying from files dealing with resource location and mapping files. There were several from the director’s personal account and a couple from management communications files. Five more minutes gave me the time to quickly look at the smaller files and showed only files dealing with setbacks in accessing some of the more remote resource pockets. The references were loaded with innuendoes and euphemisms about something, but there wasn’t enough to figure out what it was.

It took an additional twenty minutes to back out while enabling the standard setup on the way. There were probably enough clues for a good security analyst to realize the system had been compromised, but it would be unlikely that the company people here would manage to find anything. It was a quick crawl back to Kara and then a short walk back to the security station. "There you go. Everything should be back to normal now. You might want to check it before we go..." for a moment it looked like he might actually do so, then he just shrugged and waved us out.

Once we were clear of the administration complex and walking down a relatively empty street I turned to answer her unspoken question, "Someone was into the system ahead of us. They used an old, commercially available cracking system and forgot to turn on half the security procedures when they exited. If I’d left it that way someone would have noticed it for sure. What I couldn’t figure out was what they got. They weren’t in the financial sections and they weren’t in the restricted development and resource locating databases. They were scanning memos and general letters."
