Things to remember throughout

Want to aspire to a more BSG kind of intensity - meaning - need to increase and add tensions between various characters. Even the ones the generally agree on needed actions will disagree on particulars, or purposes. Those that disagree will have even more intense disagreements

figure out how the 3 sisters get along and how their roles really work in the mulrti-verse - they are the gods, but what can they do, how much do they know, and how hard can they twist on things without action/reaction/effects spinning farther out of control than the desired results

remember the Razaks and Zeltropians both attempted genocide - neither was successful, however, both significantly reduced each other's populations.

Breaks in the truce coincide with increased threats of release of the Creator and Destructor. As the multi-verse destabilizes, everything goes into flux. Aztol's capture of Ishra brought focus to the instability by shifting the balance of power in the Abyss. His goals are personal power, not the release of the Old Ones, but his actions have galvanized many factions into increased action.

Remeber that every faction has its own agenda and these will collide in unexpected ways. Take a page from Stross Merchant Prince eries in everyone has their own trajectory and this will spin their reactions to others. Things should build to a pretty messed up state before the truce is re-estabilshed, Ishra freed, and Aztol defeated.

Remember disease and sanitation dynamics in the various large cities. Some would be prone to outbreaks, smells, and other useful annoyances.

REMEMBER - the darker side of the multi-verse. This is not a multi-verse of only fluffy bunnies and kittens, there are dark forces at work as well. Think in a Lovecraftian way about some of this, besides the socio/psychopathic nature of people like Aztol.

General Ideas

Surplus shop that buys less than idea majikal and alchemical devices, then sells the to the unsuspecting populace. These items are sub-standard or flawed in one or more ways, sometimes dangerous, sometimes humorous, but never quite working as advertised. This is based off this fragment from RPGNow.

The world's ultimate goblin entrepreneur is back! His previous business (detailed in Loot 4 Less, vol. 7: Krazy Kragnar's Used Chariots) was closed after a dwarven nobleman perished in a freak houda accident. Now Krazy Kragnar returns with a whole new business plan-he is the owner and chief salesman of the Alchemical Surplus Shop, a place that buys alchemist fire that's past its use-by date, thunderstones that don't meet military specifications, and refurbished tanglefoot bags in bulk and passes the savings on to the customer.

Of course, when dealing with alchemical materials that are left over from, rejected by, or bought at the estate sale of the previous owner, you often end up with some nonstandard merchandise. The Alchemical Surplus Shop would be happy to sell discounted acid flasks and sunrods if they were available, but such items are bought up long before Kragnar gets on the scene. Not to worry, though! Less popular items are usually passed over by merchants salvaging the smoking crater of a failed alchemical bazaar, and their loss is your gain! Kragnar has pulled together new items-from the cacophonous boompot to the subtle clocktinder-all available only here!

So come browse the shelves, flip through the seasonal catalog, and check out the daily specials. Krazy Kragnar has dozens of alchemical items you've never even heard of, ready to arm adventurers with the power of almost-magic and challenge alchemists by offering new items for them to craft.
